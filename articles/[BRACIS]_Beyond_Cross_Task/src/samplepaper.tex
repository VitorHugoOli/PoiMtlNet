\begin{abstract}
We introduce \emph{Check2HGI}, a graph embedding for Point-of-Interest
(POI) prediction that produces one vector per user check-in instead of
one fixed vector per place, so the same caf\'e visited in the morning
and in the evening feeds the model with two different inputs. In
Multi-Task Learning (MTL) over Location-Based Social Networks (LBSNs),
recent work reports only modest joint gains when next-category and
next-region prediction share a graph embedding, suggesting that the
embedding, not the loss-balancing strategy, is the bottleneck. Across
five state splits of the Gowalla dataset, per-visit context lifts
category macro-F1 by 14--29 percentage points at every state, while
joint multi-task training improves category accuracy at the cost of
region accuracy, the classic multi-task tradeoff.

\keywords{Multi-Task Learning \and Point-of-Interest Prediction \and Graph
Embeddings.}
\end{abstract}
