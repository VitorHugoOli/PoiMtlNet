\begin{abstract}
Multi-task learning (MTL) is widely used for jointly training related
prediction tasks, assuming a shared representation transfers signal
between tasks and lifts both. In POI prediction over LBSNs,
recent attempts to jointly train
next-category and next-region prediction over a shared graph embedding
show only modest joint gains, suggesting the embedding, not the MTL
recipe, is the central factor. Prior responses kept the embedding's
emission rate fixed (one vector per place) and varied which features were
encoded. We instead change the emission rate, from per-place to per-visit,
so that two visits to the same place at different times can produce
different inputs. We propose Check2HGI, a check-in-level contextual graph
embedding, and validate it on five Gowalla state splits: per-visit context
lifts next-category macro-F1 by 14 to 29 percentage points at every state,
while joint multi-task training then exhibits the textbook
category-vs-region tradeoff.

\keywords{Multi-Task Learning \and Point-of-Interest Prediction \and Graph
Embeddings.}
\end{abstract}
