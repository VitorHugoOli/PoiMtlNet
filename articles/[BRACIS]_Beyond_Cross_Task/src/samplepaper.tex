\begin{abstract}
Multi-task learning for point-of-interest prediction typically pairs joint
next-category and next-region heads over a shared graph embedding,
assuming joint training transfers signal across tasks. We introduce
Check2HGI, a check-in-level contextual graph embedding extending HGI's
hierarchical contrastive objective with per-visit emission rate, and run
a controlled five-state Gowalla ablation under user-disjoint folds. The
substrate is task-asymmetric: per-visit context lifts single-task
next-category macro-F1 by 14 to 29 percentage points at every state
(paired Wilcoxon p of 0.0312), while per-place embeddings tie or
marginally exceed it under matched-head ceilings on next-region. Joint
multi-task training over a cross-attention backbone then delivers a
category lift at four of five states; the cost is a sign-consistent 7 to
17 percentage point drop on next-region top-10 accuracy, the textbook
tradeoff. A per-visit counterfactual at Alabama and Arizona attributes
about 72 and 64 percent of the substrate gain to per-visit context.

\keywords{Multi-Task Learning \and Point-of-Interest Prediction \and Graph
Embeddings.}
\end{abstract}
