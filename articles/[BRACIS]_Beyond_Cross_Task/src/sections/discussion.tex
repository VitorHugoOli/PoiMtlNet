\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

The headline reading is that the multi-task picture is dominated by the \emph{substrate's task-asymmetric value}, not cross-task transfer through joint training. Per-visit context is paper-grade significant for next-category at every state ($+14$ to $+29$ pp matched-head); on next-region the same substrate ties or marginally trails per-place HGI by $1.6$ to $3.1$ pp under the matched-head single-task ceiling, with TOST $\delta=2$ pp passing at California and Texas. With the substrate fixed, the cross-attention multi-task backbone adds a small category lift at four of five states (Arizona, Florida, California, Texas) and is small-significantly negative at Alabama ($\Delta=-0.78$ pp, $p=0.036$, $n=20$, magnitude under $2\,\%$ relative); on next-region it pays a sign-consistent $7$ to $17$ pp cost on Acc@10 at every state. The conventional reading where joint training transfers signal to lift both heads does not hold on the harder task: the category lift survives the substrate ablation, and the region cost survives the substrate fix. The substrate's task-asymmetric value is large in absolute terms --- the matched-head STL category ceiling on Check2HGI ($67.16$ at FL) roughly doubles the strongest user-disjoint POI-RGNN reproduction ($34.49$) --- and is recoverable by any downstream architecture that consumes per-visit context. The honest reattribution is substrate first (task-asymmetric), multi-task coupling second (tradeoff-paying).

Four limitations apply. The per-visit decomposition replicates at AL+AZ but is not generalised to the remaining three states, and the drop-in panel ran at Florida only. On Acc@10 the multi-task backbone trails the Markov-1-region floor at four of five states (\S\ref{subsec:results-mtl-stl}); we read this as a structural cost of joint training on the harder head rather than a Florida-specific saturation, and report $\Delta_m$ MRR alongside (FL $+2.33\,\%$, $n=25$, paper-grade positive). External coverage is asymmetric: ReHDM~\cite{li2025rehdm} is reported at FL only above the headline scale and a literature-faithful STAN reproduction is omitted due to page constraints (Table~\ref{tab:external} caption). The substrate is transductive at the state level (\S\ref{sec:setup}); held-out-user substrate retraining, cross-task transfer (e.g.\ next-POI), and architectural extensions for the region head are left to follow-up work.
