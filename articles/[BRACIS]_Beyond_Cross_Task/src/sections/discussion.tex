\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

The headline reading is that the multi-task picture is dominated by the \emph{substrate's task-asymmetric value}, not cross-task transfer through joint training. Per-visit context is paper-grade significant for next-category at every state ($+14.5$ to $+29$ pp matched-head); on next-region the same substrate ties or marginally trails per-place HGI by $1.6$ to $3.1$ pp under the matched-head single-task ceiling, with TOST $\delta=2$ pp passing at California and Texas. With the substrate fixed, the cross-attention multi-task backbone adds a small category lift at four of five states (Arizona, Florida, California, Texas) and is small-significantly negative at Alabama ($\Delta=-0.78$ pp, $p=0.036$, $n=20$, magnitude under $2\,\%$ relative); on next-region it pays a sign-consistent $7$ to $17$ pp cost on Acc@10 at every state. The conventional reading where joint training transfers signal to lift both heads does not hold on the harder task: the category lift survives the substrate ablation, and the region cost survives the substrate fix. The honest reattribution is substrate first (task-asymmetric), multi-task coupling second (tradeoff-paying).

Four limitations apply. The per-visit decomposition replicates at Alabama and Arizona but does not generalise the exact split to the remaining three states. At Florida the next-region task sits in a Markov-saturated Acc@10 regime: the Markov-1-region floor reaches $65.05$ Acc@10 and the multi-task backbone trails it on Acc@10 ($63.27$) while remaining paper-grade positive on $\Delta_m$ MRR ($+2.33\,\%$, $n=25$); we report MRR alongside. The drop-in panel ran at Florida only and cites sign-consistency across the five states as supporting context, not as a five-state ablation. External coverage is asymmetric: ReHDM~\cite{li2025rehdm} is reported at Florida only above the headline scale (its dual-level hypergraph scales quadratically with region cardinality, exceeding our compute budget at California and Texas); our POI-RGNN~\cite{capanema2022poirgnn} reproduction enforces user-disjoint folds where the published evaluation used non-user-disjoint folds, so absolute gaps to the original numbers are not directly comparable. The substrate is transductive at the state level (Section~\ref{sec:setup}); a held-out-user substrate retraining audit is left to follow-up work.
