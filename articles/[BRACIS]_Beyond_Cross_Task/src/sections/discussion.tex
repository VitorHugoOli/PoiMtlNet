\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

Overall, the multi-task picture is dominated by the substrate's asymmetric value across the two tasks, not by cross-task transfer through joint training. Per-visit context is statistically significant for next-category at every state ($+14$ to $+29$ pp matched-head); on next-region the same substrate ties or marginally trails per-place HGI ($1.6$ to $3.1$ pp; TOST $\delta=2$ pp passes at CA and TX). With the substrate fixed, joint training adds a small category lift at four of five states (the AL contrast is statistically significant but under $2\,\%$ relative) and pays a sign-consistent $7$ to $17$ pp cost on next-region Acc@10. The conventional reading where joint training transfers signal to lift both heads does not hold on the harder task: the category lift survives the substrate ablation, and the region cost survives the substrate fix. The substrate's value is also large in absolute terms (matched-head STL on Check2HGI reaches $67.16$ at FL, roughly doubling the strongest user-disjoint POI-RGNN reproduction at $34.49$) and is recoverable by any downstream architecture that consumes per-visit context. The substrate comes first; the multi-task coupling pays the textbook tradeoff on top.

Four limitations apply. The per-visit decomposition replicates at AL+AZ but is not generalised to the remaining three states, and the drop-in panel ran at Florida only. On Acc@10 the multi-task backbone trails the Markov-1-region floor at four of five states (\S\ref{subsec:results-mtl-stl}); we read this as a structural cost of joint training on the harder head rather than a Florida-specific saturation, and report $\Delta_m$ MRR alongside (FL $+2.33\,\%$, $n=25$, highly significant). External coverage is asymmetric: ReHDM~\cite{li2025rehdm} is reported at FL only above the headline scale and a literature-faithful STAN reproduction is omitted due to page constraints (Table~\ref{tab:external} caption). The substrate is transductive at the state level (\S\ref{sec:setup}); held-out-user substrate retraining, cross-task transfer (e.g.\ next-POI), and architectural extensions for the region head are left to follow-up work.
