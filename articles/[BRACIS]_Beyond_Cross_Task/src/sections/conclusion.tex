\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We introduced Check2HGI, a check-in-level contextual graph embedding, and tested whether it carries the multi-task POI prediction win across five U.S.-state Gowalla splits. The substrate is asymmetric across the two tasks: per-visit context lifts matched-head next-category macro-F1 by $+14$ to $+29$ pp at every state, with the AL/AZ counterfactual attributing $\sim$72\,\% and $\sim$64\,\% of the gap to per-visit context, while per-place embeddings tie or marginally exceed Check2HGI on next-region. With the substrate fixed, joint multi-task training adds a small category lift at four of five states and pays a $7$ to $17$ pp region cost at every state; drop-in balancers and a head-side fix do not close the gap. Three open lines follow: asymmetric routing across the shared backbone to relax cross-attention interference, encoder enrichment fusing per-visit contrast with the explicit spatial-temporal sub-encoders of~\cite{paiva2026courb}, and a region-side dynamic prior conditioned on the user's recent trajectory.
