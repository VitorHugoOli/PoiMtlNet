\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

We describe the substrate (adopted), the backbone (the object of study), and the training protocol. Notation: a check-in is $c=(p,r,t)$ with POI $p$, region $r$, timestamp $t$; trajectories split into non-overlapping length-nine windows predicting the category and region of the tenth check-in ($L=9$).

\subsection{The Check2HGI substrate}
\label{subsec:method-substrate}

Check2HGI extends the hierarchical graph-infomax line of HGI~\cite{huang2023hgi} (built on DGI~\cite{velickovic2019dgi}) by inserting a fourth check-in level beneath the POI level: the hierarchy becomes Check-in $\to$ POI $\to$ Region $\to$ City, so the same place visited twice can yield two distinct vectors. Bottom-level nodes are individual visits, with features encoding the category and a sin/cos transform of the timestamp; edges follow each user's chronological sequence with exponential temporal decay. A two-layer GCN encodes check-ins; a multi-head attention pool (\emph{Checkin2POI}) aggregates check-ins per POI; an analogous pool plus a region-adjacency GCN produces region embeddings; an area-weighted sigmoid aggregation produces the city embedding.

Training maximises mutual information across the three adjacent boundaries by bilinear discriminators against random-shuffle negatives, with a per-boundary loss decomposition
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{L}_{\text{C2HGI}} = \alpha_{c2p}\,\mathcal{L}_{c2p} + \alpha_{p2r}\,\mathcal{L}_{p2r} + \alpha_{r2c}\,\mathcal{L}_{r2c},
  \quad (\alpha_{c2p}, \alpha_{p2r}, \alpha_{r2c}) = (0.4, 0.3, 0.3),
  \label{eq:c2hgi}
\end{equation}
where each $\mathcal{L}_b$ is a binary cross-entropy against random-shuffle negatives at boundary $b$. Negatives are sampled as a permutation of positive embeddings rather than a re-encode of feature-corrupted inputs, preserving the contrastive objective while halving encoder compute.

Substrates are pre-trained per state on the full state-level check-in graph; the user-disjoint protocol applies to the downstream classifier and the per-fold transition prior, not to the substrate's exposure (\S\ref{subsec:setup-data}). Two products feed our backbone after the encoder is frozen, both 64-dimensional: a per-check-in vector $\mathbf{e}_{c}$ from the bottom level for the category stream, and a per-region vector $\mathbf{e}_{r}$ from the third level for the region stream.

\subsection{Cross-attention multi-task backbone}
\label{subsec:method-backbone}

The backbone keeps two parallel input streams bridged by cross-attention. Per-task encoders (two-layer MLPs with LayerNorm and dropout) project the input streams $X^{c}, X^{r} \in \mathbb{R}^{L\times d}$ into $H^{c}, H^{r} \in \mathbb{R}^{L\times d_m}$ ($L=9$, $d=64$, $d_m=256$). With $\mathrm{MHA}(Q,K,V)$ scaled dot-product attention ($h=8$ heads),
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{H}^{c} = H^{c} + \mathrm{MHA}(H^{c},\, H^{r},\, H^{r}),
\qquad
\widetilde{H}^{r} = H^{r} + \mathrm{MHA}(H^{r},\, H^{c},\, H^{c}),
\label{eq:crossattn}
\end{equation}
so each stream queries the other. The cross-attended streams pass through a four-block shared residual backbone (LayerNorm, linear, LeakyReLU, dropout) before splitting into per-task heads (Figure~\ref{fig:arch}).

The category head is a two-layer GRU over the cross-attended check-in stream with attention pooling and a seven-class softmax. The region head is an additive-prior STAN~\cite{luo2021stan} variant: a STAN backbone over the cross-attended region stream, scored against learnable region embeddings, with an additive log-transition prior $\alpha\cdot\log T[r_{\text{last}}]$ (learnable $\alpha$, built per fold from training-fold edges only). The log-prior pattern follows GETNext~\cite{yang2022getnext}, but our head omits friendship and check-in graph priors and is not a faithful reproduction. The single-task ceilings reuse these head classes, so multi-task and single-task rows differ only in the shared backbone above them.

\subsection{Training protocol}
\label{subsec:method-training}

We aggregate the two task losses with a static weighting,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L} = \lambda_{\text{cat}}\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{cat}} + \lambda_{\text{reg}}\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}},
\qquad
\lambda_{\text{cat}} = 0.75,\quad \lambda_{\text{reg}} = 0.25,
\label{eq:loss}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{L}_{\text{cat}}, \mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}}$ are unweighted cross-entropy, fixed across all MTL rows. The drop-in adaptive balancers in \S\ref{sec:mechanism} (FAMO~\cite{liu2023famo}, Aligned-MTL~\cite{senushkin2023aligned}) do not improve over this static choice, supporting the simpler weighting; a $\lambda_{\text{cat}}$ sensitivity sweep is in the anonymous code release.

The optimiser is AdamW with three parameter groups (category, region, shared); the schedule is scale-conditional, with OneCycleLR plus per-task-group alternating updates at FL/CA/TX and a per-head constant learning rate at AL/AZ (the alternating step helps the region head out of its saturation regime at large transition graphs but costs category-side signal at smaller scales). Batch size is 2048 (1024 at the largest state) with gradient accumulation of two. Training runs 50 epochs in FP32; per-task best checkpoints select on validation Acc@10 (region) and macro-F1 (category) for epochs $\geq 5$, since the region head saturates before the category head converges. Both heads use unweighted cross-entropy, matching the single-task ceilings in Section~\ref{sec:setup}. Exact recipe settings per state are in the anonymous code release.

Folds, seeds, and multi-seed pooling follow the user-disjoint StratifiedGroupKFold setup of Section~\ref{sec:setup}; the same splits are reused across substrates, heads, and recipes so paired tests are well defined. \textbf{A methodological caveat:} under cross-attention, a loss-side ablation that zeros one task's weight does not isolate the targeted encoder, since it co-adapts through the attention key and value projections of the surviving stream. We use encoder-frozen isolation (random-init, frozen) as the clean architectural decomposition throughout; Section~\ref{subsec:mechanism-isolation} develops the note. Code, fold splits, seeds, per-fold transition priors, and regression tests are released at \url{https://anonymous.4open.science/r/PoiMtlNet-FE6A/}.
