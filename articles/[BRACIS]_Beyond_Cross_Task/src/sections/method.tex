\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

We describe the substrate (adopted), the backbone (the object of study), and the training protocol. Notation: a check-in is $c=(p,r,t)$ with POI $p$, region $r$, timestamp $t$; trajectories split into non-overlapping length-nine windows predicting the category and region of the tenth check-in ($L=9$).

\subsection{The Check2HGI substrate}
\label{subsec:method-substrate}

Check2HGI extends the hierarchical graph-infomax line of HGI~\cite{huang2023hgi} (built on DGI~\cite{velickovic2019dgi}) by inserting a check-in level beneath the POI level: the hierarchy becomes Check-in $\to$ POI $\to$ Region $\to$ City. Bottom-level nodes are individual visits; edges follow each user's chronological visit sequence with a temporal-decay weighting, and node features carry the visit's category and timestamp. Embeddings propagate upward by attention pooling (check-ins to POIs), a region-graph convolution, and area-weighted aggregation (regions to city); mutual information is maximised across the three adjacent boundaries via bilinear discriminators against random-shuffle negatives. Two products feed our backbone after the encoder is frozen, both 64-dimensional: a per-check-in vector $\mathbf{e}_{c}$ from the bottom level for the category stream, and a per-region vector $\mathbf{e}_{r}$ from the third level for the region stream. We treat Check2HGI as a fixed input substrate; the full recipe is in supplementary material.

\subsection{Cross-attention multi-task backbone}
\label{subsec:method-backbone}

The backbone keeps two parallel input streams (one per task) and bridges them with cross-attention before a shared backbone splits into per-task heads. Let $X^{c}, X^{r}\in\mathbb{R}^{L\times d}$ feed the category and region sides at $L=9$, $d=64$. Two task-specific encoders (two-layer MLPs with LayerNorm and dropout) project each stream to $\mathbb{R}^{L\times d_m}$ at $d_m=256$, yielding $H^{c}$ and $H^{r}$.

A cross-attention block bridges the streams. With $\mathrm{MHA}(Q,K,V)$ scaled dot-product attention ($h=8$),
\begin{equation}
\widetilde{H}^{c} = H^{c} + \mathrm{MHA}(H^{c},\, H^{r},\, H^{r}),
\qquad
\widetilde{H}^{r} = H^{r} + \mathrm{MHA}(H^{r},\, H^{c},\, H^{c}),
\label{eq:crossattn}
\end{equation}
so each stream queries the other. The cross-attended streams then pass through a shared backbone of four residual blocks (LayerNorm, linear, LeakyReLU, dropout) before splitting into per-task heads (Figure~\ref{fig:arch}). Eq.~\eqref{eq:crossattn} is the architectural object the result tables interrogate.

The category head is a two-layer GRU over the cross-attended check-in stream with attention pooling and a seven-class softmax. The region head is an additive-prior STAN~\cite{luo2021stan} variant: a STAN backbone over the cross-attended region stream, scored against learnable region embeddings, with an additive log-transition prior $\alpha\cdot\log T[r_{\text{last}}]$ (learnable $\alpha$, built per fold from training-fold edges only). The log-prior pattern follows GETNext, but our head omits friendship and check-in graph priors and is not a faithful reproduction. The single-task ceilings reuse these head classes, so multi-task and single-task rows differ only in the shared backbone above them.

\subsection{Training protocol}
\label{subsec:method-training}

We aggregate the two task losses with a static weighting,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L} = \lambda_{\text{cat}}\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{cat}} + \lambda_{\text{reg}}\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}},
\qquad
\lambda_{\text{cat}} = 0.75,\quad \lambda_{\text{reg}} = 0.25,
\label{eq:loss}
\end{equation}
where $\mathcal{L}_{\text{cat}}, \mathcal{L}_{\text{reg}}$ are unweighted cross-entropy, fixed across all MTL rows. FAMO~\cite{liu2023famo} and Aligned-MTL~\cite{senushkin2023aligned} drop-in comparisons are in Section~\ref{sec:mechanism}.

The optimiser is AdamW with three parameter groups (category, region, shared) and OneCycleLR, batch size 2048 (1024 at the largest state), gradient accumulation of two. Training runs 50 epochs in FP32; per-task best checkpoints select on validation Acc@10 (region) and macro-F1 (category) for epochs $\geq 5$, since the region head saturates before the category head converges. Both heads use unweighted cross-entropy, matching the single-task ceilings in Section~\ref{sec:setup}.

Folds, seeds, and multi-seed pooling follow the user-disjoint StratifiedGroupKFold setup of Section~\ref{sec:setup}; the same splits are reused across substrates, heads, and recipes so paired tests are well defined. \textbf{A methodological caveat:} under cross-attention, a loss-side ablation that zeros one task's weight does not isolate the targeted encoder, since it co-adapts through the attention key and value projections of the surviving stream. We use encoder-frozen isolation (random-init, frozen) as the clean architectural decomposition throughout; Section~\ref{subsec:mechanism-isolation} develops the note. Code, fold splits, seeds, per-fold transition priors, and regression tests are released at \url{https://anonymous.4open.science/r/PoiMtlNet-FE6A/}.
