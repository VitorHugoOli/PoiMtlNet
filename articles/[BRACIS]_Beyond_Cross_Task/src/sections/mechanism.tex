\section{Mechanism and Ablations}
\label{sec:mechanism}

This section turns the reattribution into a mechanism reading, stress-tests the next-region cost against drop-in alternatives, and closes with a methodological note. Section~\ref{subsec:mechanism-pervisit} isolates per-visit context at one state. Section~\ref{subsec:mechanism-ablations} reports adaptive balancers and a head-side fix that fail to close the gap. Section~\ref{subsec:mechanism-isolation} states the cross-attention loss-side pitfall and the encoder-frozen protocol that decomposes it cleanly.

\subsection{Per-visit context counterfactual at Alabama}
\label{subsec:mechanism-pervisit}

We isolate per-visit context through a pooled-vs-canonical counterfactual at Alabama. POI-pooled \emph{Check2HGI} mean-pools canonical vectors per POI across all check-ins and applies the per-place result uniformly to every visit. This kills per-visit variation while preserving the training signal: canonical vs.\ pooled isolates per-visit context, pooled vs.\ HGI isolates the contrastive training signal.

Under the matched-head GRU single-task ceiling at AL, canonical Check2HGI reaches $40.76$ macro-F1, POI-pooled reaches $29.57$, HGI reaches $25.26$ (Figure~\ref{fig:per-visit}). The per-visit component is $+11.19$ pp ($\sim$72\,\% of the substrate gap), the training-signal component $+4.31$ pp ($\sim$28\,\%). The split survives a head-free linear probe ($30.84$ vs.\ $23.20$ vs.\ $18.70$, per-visit share $\sim$63\,\%); the GRU head amplifies the per-visit share rather than creating it. Per-visit variance is what the category head exploits; pooling to per-place vectors (HGI) or to region cardinality (region head) smooths the same variance away, consistent with the asymmetry in Section~\ref{subsec:results-substrate}.

This is single-state evidence; we do not generalise the $\sim$72\,\% / $\sim$28\,\% split to the other four states, and a second-state replication is left to future work. The mechanism nonetheless explains \emph{why} the substrate carries the easier head and trails on the harder one: what it adds at AL is exactly what the region head does not need.

\subsection{Drop-in MTL ablations do not recover the next-region cost}
\label{subsec:mechanism-ablations}

The next-region cost is sign-consistent across all five states ($7$ to $17$ pp on Acc@10). To probe whether the cost is structural or recipe-borne, we ran three drop-in replacements at FL against the same folds and seeds: a magnitude balancer (FAMO~\cite{liu2023famo}), a direction-aligning balancer (Aligned-MTL~\cite{senushkin2023aligned}), and a head-side fix (hierarchical-additive-softmax region head). Each is drop-in: substrate, backbone, optimiser, and schedule fixed at Section~\ref{sec:method}. None reaches a $+3$ pp Acc@10 recovery target at FL, and none is paired-Wilcoxon significant at $p_{\text{greater}}<0.10$ (FAMO closes $+0.62$ pp; Aligned-MTL ties at $-0.11$ pp on next-region and regresses $-0.90$ pp on next-category; the hierarchical head reaches $\approx +1.7$ pp dropping a single collapsed fold-init). Across the three replacements the next-region cost stays effectively intact, suggesting it is structural to the cross-attention interaction with the region head rather than an artefact of the loss recipe. The panel ran at FL only; we cite the sign-consistency of the cost across the five states as supporting context, not as direct multi-state evidence.

\subsection{Cross-attention $\lambda = 0$ is not encoder isolation}
\label{subsec:mechanism-isolation}

A natural ablation zeros one task's weight in Eq.~\eqref{eq:loss}, on the reading that $\lambda_{\text{cat}}=0$ or $\lambda_{\text{reg}}=0$ ablates the corresponding encoder. Under cross-attention this is unsound: the targeted encoder still updates through the key/value projections of the surviving stream, so its parameters co-adapt to the surviving task. We therefore use \textbf{encoder-frozen isolation} (targeted encoder random-init, frozen) as the clean architectural decomposition. The note generalises to any cross-task attention multi-task model that ablates by zeroing a task weight; regression tests are in our anonymous code release.
