\section{Mechanism and Ablations}
\label{sec:mechanism}

This section turns the reattribution into a mechanism reading, stress-tests the next-region cost against drop-in alternatives, and closes with a methodological note on cross-attention loss-side ablations.

\subsection{Per-visit context counterfactual (AL + AZ)}
\label{subsec:mechanism-pervisit}

To isolate per-visit context, we compare canonical Check2HGI against POI-pooled Check2HGI (canonical vectors mean-pooled per POI and applied uniformly to every visit, killing per-visit variation while preserving the training signal). Canonical vs.\ pooled isolates per-visit context; pooled vs.\ HGI isolates the contrastive training signal.

Under the matched-head GRU single-task ceiling, the substrate gap (canonical $-$ HGI) splits cleanly at both states (Figure~\ref{fig:per-visit}): per-visit dominates at AL ($\sim$72\,\%, $+11.19$ pp of $+15.50$) and AZ ($\sim$64\,\%, $+9.08$ of $+14.18$); the training signal contributes the remainder. Both per-visit and training-signal deltas are paired-Wilcoxon positive at each state ($p=0.0312$, $5/5$ folds, $n=5$ ceiling), so AZ replicates AL inferentially rather than descriptively. A head-free linear probe at AL gives a comparable per-visit share ($\sim$63\,\%; the GRU head amplifies the share rather than creating it).

\subsection{Drop-in MTL ablations do not recover the next-region cost}
\label{subsec:mechanism-ablations}

The next-region cost is sign-consistent across all five states ($7$ to $17$ pp on Acc@10). At FL we tried three drop-in replacements on the same folds and seeds with substrate, backbone, optimiser and schedule fixed at \S\ref{sec:method}: a magnitude balancer (FAMO~\cite{liu2023famo}), a direction-aligning balancer (Aligned-MTL~\cite{senushkin2023aligned}) and a hierarchical-additive-softmax region head. None reached a $+3$ pp Acc@10 recovery target or paired-Wilcoxon $p_{\text{greater}}<0.10$ (FAMO $+0.62$, Aligned-MTL $-0.11$ on reg and $-0.90$ on cat, hierarchical head $\approx +1.7$). The cost stays intact, suggesting it is structural to the cross-attention interaction with the region head rather than a loss-recipe artefact; five-state sign-consistency is supporting context, not direct multi-state evidence.

\subsection{Cross-attention $\lambda = 0$ is not encoder isolation}
\label{subsec:mechanism-isolation}

A natural ablation zeros one task's weight in Eq.~\eqref{eq:loss}, on the reading that $\lambda_{\text{cat}}=0$ or $\lambda_{\text{reg}}=0$ ablates the corresponding encoder. Under cross-attention this is unsound: the targeted encoder still updates through the key/value projections of the surviving stream, so its parameters co-adapt to the surviving task. A targeted probe at AZ on the per-head constant LR recipe quantifies this: encoder-frozen isolation reaches region Acc@10 $60.72$, while the loss-side $\lambda_{\text{cat}}=0$ ablation reaches $62.70$ on the same folds and seeds --- a $+1.98$ pp gap the surviving stream drives through the targeted encoder's K/V projections. We therefore use \textbf{encoder-frozen isolation} (targeted encoder random-init, frozen) as the clean architectural decomposition; the note generalises to any cross-task attention multi-task model that ablates by zeroing a task weight, and regression tests are in our anonymous code release.
