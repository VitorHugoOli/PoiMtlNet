\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Location-Based Social Networks (LBSNs) record user check-ins at scale, motivating two recurring Point-of-Interest (POI) prediction tasks: the next visit's semantic category, which feeds content-aware recommendation and intent inference, and its spatial region, which feeds demand forecasting and short-horizon mobility planning. The two tasks place disjoint demands. \textbf{Category prediction} is a coarse semantic decision shaped by per-visit context (intent, time-of-day, co-location). \textbf{Region prediction} is a fine-grained spatial ranking that hinges on spatial-temporal regularities and short-horizon transitions. The multi-task learning (MTL) appeal is standard: a shared representation should let one head's signal transfer and lift both~\cite{caruana1997multitask,vandenhende2022mtl}.

Two recent works frame the bottleneck. The first reported hard-parameter-sharing MTL on POI-stable graph embeddings (DGI~\cite{velickovic2019dgi}) yielding only marginal gains within standard deviations; the diagnosis was representation mismatch~\cite{silva2025mtlnet}. A follow-up decomposed the monolithic embedding into spatial, temporal, and categorical sub-encoders, recovering category-side gains across three states~\cite{paiva2026courb}. Together these suggest embedding choice, not the MTL recipe, is the central factor.

Both prior responses treat the embedding as a \emph{what} axis. We test an orthogonal \emph{granularity} axis: emission rate. POI-stable embeddings like HGI~\cite{huang2023hgi} emit one vector per place; a check-in-level variant emits one per visit, capturing intent shifts across two visits to the same caf\'e at different hours. Our hypothesis is that \textbf{per-visit context} is the load-bearing substrate property for the easier head, and that once the substrate is fixed, joint training behaves like the textbook MTL tradeoff. The carry is task-asymmetric: per-visit \emph{carries} next-category; per-place embeddings tie or marginally exceed it on next-region.

We adopt Check2HGI as a check-in-level extension of the hierarchical graph-infomax line and run a controlled ablation on five Gowalla~\cite{cho2011gowalla} U.S.-state splits, Alabama (AL), Arizona (AZ), Florida (FL), California (CA) and Texas (TX), under user-disjoint folds (transductive substrate; \S\ref{subsec:setup-data}). We compare Check2HGI against HGI under matched-head single-task (STL) ceilings, and run a cross-attention multi-task backbone whose per-task heads match the ceiling classes so that any difference between multi-task and single-task rows is attributable to the sharing architecture rather than head capacity. A per-visit counterfactual decomposes the substrate gain itself; the architectural details, the matched-head construction, and an encoder-frozen isolation protocol that addresses a cross-attention pitfall are deferred to Sections~\ref{sec:method}--\ref{sec:mechanism}.

The main contributions of this paper are as follows:
\begin{itemize}
    \item C0. \emph{Check2HGI}, a check-in-level contextual graph embedding extending HGI's hierarchical contrastive objective with a fourth emission level (Check-in $\to$ POI $\to$ Region $\to$ City), where the same place visited twice can yield two distinct vectors.
    \item C1. A controlled five-state matched-head ablation that separates substrate from head capacity. Check2HGI lifts single-task category macro-F1 over per-place HGI by $+14$ to $+29$ percentage points (pp) at every state (paired Wilcoxon $p = 0.0312$ each, 5/5 folds positive); on next-region the same substrate ties or marginally trails per-place embeddings. A pooled-vs-canonical counterfactual replicates at Alabama and Arizona, attributing $\sim$72\,\% and $\sim$64\,\% of the category gap to per-visit context.
    \item C2. A direct characterisation of the multi-task tradeoff under the substrate established by C1, including drop-in balancers and a head-side fix that do not close the next-region gap --- indicating a structural cost of the cross-attention shared-backbone interaction with the region head, not a loss-recipe artefact. With the substrate fixed, joint cross-attention multi-task training adds a small category lift at four of five states and pays a sign-consistent $7$ to $17$ pp cost on next-region Acc@10 at every state.
\end{itemize}
