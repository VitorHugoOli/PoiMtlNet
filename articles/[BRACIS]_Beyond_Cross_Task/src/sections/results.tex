\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We report the empirical reattribution across five Gowalla state splits. Section~\ref{subsec:results-substrate} isolates the substrate axis (Check2HGI vs HGI under matched-head STL ceilings). Section~\ref{subsec:results-mtl-stl} compares the cross-attention MTL backbone against those ceilings, the headline contrast. Section~\ref{subsec:results-deltam} reports the joint $\Delta_m$ score. Section~\ref{subsec:results-external} situates the model against external baselines.

\subsection{Substrate ablation: per-visit context is task-asymmetric}
\label{subsec:results-substrate}

Table~\ref{tab:substrate}(a) reports next-category macro-F1 under the matched-head GRU single-task ceiling, with substrate as the only changing factor. Check2HGI exceeds HGI by $+14.52$ to $+29.02$ percentage points (pp) at every state (smallest at AZ, largest at FL). The paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test reaches the $n=5$ ceiling everywhere ($p=0.0312$, 5/5 folds positive). Two robustness probes back this: the AL/AZ gap is head-invariant across linear, single-layer, GRU, and LSTM probes, and the FL/CA/TX gap replicates under the matched-head ceiling. We read the lift as evidence that per-visit context, not POI-level attribute encoding, is what the next-category head exploits.

Table~\ref{tab:substrate}(b) reports Acc@10 under the matched-head STAN-based single-task ceiling. POI-stable HGI is nominally above Check2HGI by 1.6 to 3.1 pp at every state; on FL/CA/TX the gap shrinks to 1.6 to 2.1 pp and TOST non-inferiority at $\delta=2$ pp passes at CA and TX. At FL the $\delta=3$ pp test passes. AL and AZ stay outside both margins. The short-horizon transition structure driving the region head is captured equally well, or fractionally better, by per-place embeddings whose category-level pooling smooths the per-visit variance that helps the category head.

The substrate's effect is task-asymmetric. The category head reads per-visit context and lifts by an order of magnitude more than its standard-deviation envelope; the region head reads transition structure preserved under per-place pooling, tying within $\pm 2$ pp. Section~\ref{sec:mechanism} attributes the asymmetry through a per-visit-context counterfactual replicated at Alabama and Arizona. Any multi-task system on this task pair operates on a substrate that already encodes the easier task more clearly than the harder one.

\subsection{Multi-task vs single-task on the load-bearing substrate}
\label{subsec:results-mtl-stl}

Table~\ref{tab:mtl-stl} reports the headline contrast: the cross-attention multi-task backbone against the matched-head GRU and STAN-based single-task ceilings, both on Check2HGI, multi-seed pooled at $n=20$ fold-pairs per state. Category is positive at four of five states: FL $+1.40$ pp ($p=2\mathrm{e}{-6}$), CA $+1.68$ pp ($p=2\mathrm{e}{-6}$), TX $+1.89$ pp ($p=2\mathrm{e}{-6}$), AZ $+1.20$ pp ($p<1\mathrm{e}{-4}$). At AL the contrast is small-significantly negative ($\Delta=-0.78$ pp, $p=0.036$, $n=20$), under 2\,\% relative on the 41\,\% F1 scale. The four positive states sit outside the seed and fold envelope; we report the AL negative as is rather than absorbing it.

\input{tables/mtl_vs_stl.tex}

Next-region is the textbook tradeoff. MTL pays $-7.34$ pp (FL) to $-16.59$ pp (TX) on Acc@10 at every state, all paper-grade significant ($p\leq 1.9\mathrm{e}{-6}$). The pattern is broadly downward on AL\,$\to$\,AZ\,$\to$\,FL, with CA preserving the FL regime and TX breaking the monotonic shrink. We read the TX non-monotonicity descriptively: state-specific factors beyond raw class count (geographic POI distribution, user-density skew, per-state log-transition prior coverage) plausibly contribute; we do not read the AL\,$\to$\,FL contraction as an inferential scaling claim.

At FL the multi-task row sits below the Markov-1-region floor on Acc@10 ($63.27$ vs $65.05$, Table~\ref{tab:external}). The honest reading is that the short-horizon transition structure at FL's region cardinality is largely captured by a one-step Markov chain, and Acc@10 saturates near that floor for any model whose region head pays a substrate cost. The matched-head STAN-based single-task ceiling on Check2HGI sits well above the floor at $70.62$, so the cross-attention multi-task backbone is trading that ceiling for the joint regime rather than failing to clear a Markov baseline outright. The $\Delta_m$ MRR axis at FL ($+2.33\,\%$, $n=25$, paper-grade in Section~\ref{subsec:results-deltam}) characterises the joint tradeoff more cleanly than Acc@10 in this saturation regime; we report both axes and read them jointly.

With the substrate fixed at the per-visit granularity, the multi-task backbone delivers a small category lift at four of five states and pays a sign-consistent cost on the harder head at every state, the classic multi-task tradeoff. The cost is structural to the cross-attention shared-backbone interaction with the region head; Section~\ref{sec:mechanism} reports drop-in replacements (FAMO, Aligned-MTL, hierarchical-additive-softmax region head) that fail to close the next-region gap.

\subsection{Joint $\Delta_m$ score: a Pareto-positive cell at Florida}
\label{subsec:results-deltam}

We compute $\Delta_m$~\cite{maninis2019attentive,vandenhende2022mtl} against the matched-head single-task ceiling. On the primary pair (category macro-F1, region MRR), FL carries an $n=25$ multi-seed sweep and reaches $\Delta_m\text{-MRR}=+2.33\,\%$ at $p=2.98\mathrm{e}{-8}$, 25/25 fold-pairs positive: a Pareto-positive cell on the primary axis. The secondary pair at FL is $\Delta_m\text{-Acc@10}=-1.12\,\%$ at $p=3.20\mathrm{e}{-5}$ (4/25); MRR rewards near-correct candidates that raw top-10 accuracy misses. The other four states report at the $n=5$ Wilcoxon ceiling: $\Delta_m$ is negative on both axes (MRR from $-24.84\,\%$ to $-1.61\,\%$; Acc@10 from $-22.41\,\%$ to $-6.85\,\%$), at most $1/5$ fold-pairs positive. FL is the only Pareto-positive cell on the primary axis at multi-seed.

\subsection{External baselines}
\label{subsec:results-external}

Table~\ref{tab:external} situates the model against external baselines. On next-category, our matched-head single-task ceiling on Check2HGI sits well above our faithful POI-RGNN reproduction~\cite{capanema2022poirgnn}, but the gap should be read with care: the published POI-RGNN evaluation used non-user-disjoint folds, so absolute numbers are not directly comparable to our user-disjoint reproduction. The substrate-axis ablation of Section~\ref{subsec:results-substrate} attributes a substantial share of the lift to per-visit context, not to multi-task aggregation. On next-region, the faithful STAN reproduction~\cite{luo2021stan} on HGI is the strongest external baseline; per-place HGI is a more natural fit for the region head, consistent with the substrate-axis reading. Our multi-task row trails STAN-on-HGI by $-10$ to $-20$ pp on Acc@10, consistent with the substrate gap compounded by the cross-attention region cost.
