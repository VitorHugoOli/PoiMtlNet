\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

We report the empirical reattribution across five Gowalla state splits. Section~\ref{subsec:results-substrate} isolates the substrate axis (Check2HGI vs HGI under matched-head STL ceilings). Section~\ref{subsec:results-mtl-stl} compares the cross-attention MTL backbone against those ceilings, the headline contrast. Section~\ref{subsec:results-deltam} reports the joint $\Delta_m$ score. Section~\ref{subsec:results-external} situates the model against external baselines.

\subsection{Substrate ablation: per-visit context is task-asymmetric}
\label{subsec:results-substrate}

Table~\ref{tab:substrate}(a) reports next-category macro-F1 under the matched-head \texttt{next\_gru} STL ceiling, with substrate as the only changing factor. Check2HGI exceeds HGI by $+14.52$ to $+29.02$ percentage points (pp) at every state (smallest at AZ, largest at FL). The paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test reaches the $n=5$ ceiling everywhere ($p=0.0312$, 5/5 folds positive). Two robustness probes back this: the AL/AZ gap is head-invariant across linear, single-layer, GRU, and LSTM probes, and the FL/CA/TX gap replicates under the matched-head ceiling. We read the lift as evidence that per-visit context, not POI-level attribute encoding, is what the next-category head exploits.

Table~\ref{tab:substrate}(b) reports Acc@10 under the matched-head \texttt{next\_stan\_flow} STL ceiling. POI-stable HGI is nominally above Check2HGI by 1.6 to 3.1 pp at every state; on FL/CA/TX the gap shrinks to 1.6 to 2.1 pp and TOST non-inferiority at $\delta=2$ pp passes at CA and TX. At FL the $\delta=3$ pp test passes. AL and AZ stay outside both margins. The short-horizon transition structure driving the region head is captured equally well, or fractionally better, by per-place embeddings whose category-level pooling smooths the per-visit variance that helps the category head.

The substrate's effect is task-asymmetric. The category head reads per-visit context and lifts by an order of magnitude more than its standard-deviation envelope; the region head reads transition structure preserved under per-place pooling, tying within $\pm 2$ pp. Section~\ref{sec:mechanism} attributes the asymmetry through a per-visit-context counterfactual at Alabama. Any MTL system on this task pair operates on a substrate that already encodes the easier task more clearly than the harder one.

\subsection{Multi-task vs single-task on the load-bearing substrate}
\label{subsec:results-mtl-stl}

Table~\ref{tab:mtl-stl} reports the headline contrast: the cross-attention MTL backbone against the matched-head \texttt{next\_gru} and \texttt{next\_stan\_flow} STL ceilings, both on Check2HGI, multi-seed pooled at $n=20$ fold-pairs per state. Category is positive at four of five states: FL $+1.40$ pp ($p=2\mathrm{e}{-6}$), CA $+1.68$ pp ($p=2\mathrm{e}{-6}$), TX $+1.89$ pp ($p=2\mathrm{e}{-6}$), AZ $+1.20$ pp ($p<1\mathrm{e}{-4}$). At AL the contrast is small-significantly negative ($\Delta=-0.78$ pp, $p=0.036$, $n=20$), under 2\,\% relative on the 41\,\% F1 scale. The four positive states sit outside the seed and fold envelope; we report the AL negative as is rather than absorbing it.

\input{tables/mtl_vs_stl.tex}

Next-region is the textbook tradeoff. MTL pays $-7.34$ pp (FL) to $-16.59$ pp (TX) on Acc@10 at every state, all paper-grade significant ($p\leq 1.9\mathrm{e}{-6}$). The pattern is broadly downward on AL\,$\to$\,AZ\,$\to$\,FL, with CA preserving the FL regime and TX breaking the monotonic shrink. We read the TX non-monotonicity descriptively: state-specific factors beyond raw class count (geographic POI distribution, user-density skew, per-state log-transition prior coverage) plausibly contribute; we do not read the AL\,$\to$\,FL contraction as an inferential scaling claim.

With the substrate fixed at the per-visit granularity, the MTL backbone delivers a small category lift at four of five states and pays a sign-consistent cost on the harder head at every state, the classic MTL tradeoff. The cost is structural to the cross-attention shared-backbone interaction with the region head; Section~\ref{sec:mechanism} reports drop-in replacements (FAMO, Aligned-MTL, hierarchical-additive-softmax region head) that fail to close the next-region gap.

\subsection{Joint $\Delta_m$ score: a Pareto-positive cell at Florida}
\label{subsec:results-deltam}

We compute $\Delta_m$~\cite{maninis2019attentive,vandenhende2022mtl} against the matched-head STL ceiling. On the primary pair (category macro-F1, region MRR), FL carries an $n=25$ multi-seed sweep and is the strongest paired result in the paper: $\Delta_m\text{-MRR}=+2.33\,\%$ at $p=2.98\mathrm{e}{-8}$, 25/25 fold-pairs positive. The secondary pair at FL is $\Delta_m\text{-Acc@10}=-1.12\,\%$ at $p=3.20\mathrm{e}{-5}$ (4/25); MRR rewards near-correct candidates that raw top-10 accuracy misses. The other four states report at the $n=5$ Wilcoxon ceiling: $\Delta_m$ is negative on both axes ($-24.84$ to $-1.61\,\%$ MRR; $-22.41$ to $-6.85\,\%$ Acc@10), at most 1/5 fold-pairs positive. FL is the lone Pareto-positive cell on the primary axis; the other four are reported under the $n=5$ ceiling caveat.

\subsection{External baselines}
\label{subsec:results-external}

Table~\ref{tab:external} situates the model against external baselines. On next-category, our matched-head \texttt{next\_gru} STL ceiling on Check2HGI exceeds the faithful POI-RGNN reproduction~\cite{capanema2022poirgnn} by $+27.4$ pp (FL: 67.16 vs 34.49) to $+30.6$ pp (CA: 62.39 vs 31.78); our MTL row widens the gap to $+30.0$ to $+33.2$ pp. Faithful MHA+PE~\cite{zeng2019mhape} sits below POI-RGNN at all three states (FL 32.06, CA 29.13, TX 29.91). Two qualifications: published POI-RGNN~\cite{capanema2022poirgnn} used non-user-disjoint folds, so absolute gaps to the original numbers are not comparable, and our reproduction enforces user-disjoint folds. We do not read the external category gap as a state-of-the-art claim; the substrate-axis evidence attributes the bulk of it to per-visit context, not MTL aggregation.

\input{tables/external.tex}

On next-region, the faithful STAN reproduction~\cite{luo2021stan} on HGI is the strongest external baseline at all three states (FL 73.58, CA 60.45, TX 62.70 Acc@10), exceeding our STL \texttt{next\_stan\_flow} ceiling on Check2HGI (FL 70.62, CA 56.85, TX 59.44). With the substrate-ablation reading, POI-stable HGI is a more natural fit for the region head, and the per-visit substrate's region cost reflects the same property that helps the category head. Faithful ReHDM~\cite{li2025rehdm} at FL (65.68 Acc@10) sits below both STAN-on-HGI and our \texttt{next\_stan\_flow} ceiling, and below the per-fold Markov-1-region floor at AZ. Our MTL row trails STAN-on-HGI on Acc@10 by $-10.31$ pp (FL: 63.27 vs 73.58) to $-19.86$ pp (TX: 42.84 vs 62.70), consistent with the substrate gap compounded by the cross-attention reg cost.

The category gap to external baselines is large and substrate-driven; the region picture favours POI-stable embeddings under a matched-head STAN backbone, and the MTL backbone on the per-visit substrate pays a structural region cost that drop-in MTL recipes do not close (Section~\ref{sec:mechanism}).
