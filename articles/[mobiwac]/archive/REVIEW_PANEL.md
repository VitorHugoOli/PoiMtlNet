## 3. Top Weaknesses, Ranked (duplicates merged across reviewers)

**W1 — BLOCKER. The Part-2 headline claim is asserted across a regime it has not measured.** Raised independently by **all four** substantive reviewers (rigor #1, novelty implicitly via C3, clarity W1, honesty #1). CA (largest, 8,501 regions — the exact case the claim is about) is unmeasured and its "−3" is a forecast; AL/AZ non-inferiority is carried from a non-overlapping windowing; only FL (non-inferior) and TX (−2.41, *fails* the 2-pt margin) are measured under the adopted setup. A monotone "cost grows with region count" law is being drawn through two interior points plus an extrapolation at the extreme. Rigor adds the deeper cut: region *count* and check-in *density* are confounded across these states, and the mechanism ("dense data lets a dedicated region model pull ahead") is a narrative with no regression, no density control, and the encoder-isolation probe run only at the two *smallest* states where the cost does not appear. **If CA returns worse than −3, the central contribution inverts from "small predictable cost" to "joint model materially underperforms on region at scale."**

**W2 — BLOCKER. The leak rebuttal — the answer to the prior rejection — rests on a 2-state measurement with unsourced numbers and the inductive case open.** Raised by rigor (#2), honesty (#3), clarity (W3). The stride-1 overlap leak re-audit is a static code-read, with the empirical check on the adopted sequences deferred. Shipping the leak rebuttal with un-audited numbers is the single highest-stakes version of the overclaim problem: it is the paper's whole answer to R1, and it is not yet evidenced.

**W3 — BLOCKER (conditional). Novelty C1 collapses to "CTLE on a hierarchical graph," and CTLE is not yet a scored, leak-clean result.** Raised by novelty (#1, the existential threat) and rigor (#4). Per the plan's own §1, "to substantiate 'novel', the CTLE baseline must be in the results"; per the repo, CTLE is unrun (n=20 deferred to P3) and the one run it had carried an 81.8% transductive overlap. If CTLE is cut under page pressure (live risk per §11) or returns ≈ Check2HGI on next-category, **contribution 1's novelty evaporates entirely.** This is a BLOCKER because the venue is already skeptical of non-networking work and the paper cannot afford a hollow novelty claim. **W4 — MAJOR. Venue fit: stripped of motivation paragraphs, this is a generic LBSN recommendation paper with zero network/mobility-management contribution.** Raised primarily by venue-fit (the whole review), echoed by novelty's closing risk and honesty's no-testbed concern. If (ii) is the honest read, then "one model, two tasks" *mis-frames* the result — the win is a better encoder obtained at no extra deployment cost, not multi-task transfer. Presentation, not science.

# Reviewer 1 — Venue Fit & Relevance (MobiWac 2026)

**Summary (5-6 sentences).**
This is a competently designed multi-task machine-learning paper on POI prediction (next-category + next-region from Gowalla/Massive-STEPS check-ins), and the authors are admirably honest that it makes *no* network or systems contribution: mobility management appears only as motivation in the intro, problem statement, and discussion (Decision "Framing" §0; §3; §7). The technical core — a check-in-level graph representation and a single joint model that beats a dedicated category model while staying near a dedicated region model — is a representation-learning and MTL result, not a mobility-management result. The plan itself concedes the venue risk repeatedly ("This raises the framing risk… the honest trade we are making," §0; "the softer end of MobiWac's range," §10.2) and leans entirely on a 2020 Best Paper / 2021 finalist precedent plus three keyword hits in the CfP. The problem is that "motivation only" here is genuinely *only* motivation: every networking touchpoint (prefetch, handover, connection setup, capacity planning) is asserted in the conditional mood with zero modeling, zero metric, and zero link from the predicted quantities (category macro-F1, region Acc@10) to any access/handover/management objective. Stripped of the lanyard, a TPC member reads a generic LBSN recommendation paper that could go to RecSys, SIGSPATIAL, CIKM, or a mobility-data venue with no edits. I would not desk-reject on relevance grounds (the precedent is real and the CfP keywords are a fig leaf that does technically apply), but I would argue to **reject** in committee unless the authors build an actual bridge to the management problem.

---

**The 3 sharpest relevance weaknesses.**

1. **The networking motivation is unfalsifiable decoration, not a thread the paper can be evaluated on.** Across §1 ¶1, §3, and §7 the service angle is always conditional ("could mean pre-loading… getting a handover ready… setting aside a connection," §1 ¶1; "for example to pre-load content, prepare a connection, or plan capacity," §3). The plan explicitly forbids itself from ever connecting these to a number: "we do not build or evaluate such a service" (§3), "no measured number… keep it out of the contributions and the results" (§7 usage illustration). A networking reviewer cannot assess *whether next-region Acc@10 is even the right metric* for the claimed prefetch/handover use, because the paper deliberately never argues it. Worse, the claimed uses don't survive light scrutiny: a **census tract** (the explicitly chosen region unit, §3, Glossary §1) is the wrong spatial granularity for handover or cell/AP selection (those are governed by radio coverage cells, not administrative boundaries), and "Acc@10" — the true region in the top *ten* tracts — is far too loose to "set aside a connection" or "get a handover ready." So even the *motivation* is mismatched to the metric, which a hard-networking TPC will catch immediately. This is the single biggest scope liability.

2. **Nothing in the method, data, or evaluation is mobility-management-specific; the paper is venue-portable with zero edits.** The contributions (§1 ¶5) are: a check-in-level graph embedding, a joint MTL architecture with a private spatial path, and a cross-scale account of when sharing helps. None references network state, access, mobility *management* (handoff, paging, mobility models, load), latency, or any system constraint. The datasets are social check-ins (Gowalla, Massive-STEPS), not mobility traces with network/handover semantics (e.g., cellular CDRs, trajectory-with-cell-association, RAN logs). The metrics (macro-F1, Acc@10) are pure prediction quality. Per the CfP keywords the authors lean on — "AI-based mobility management," "Mobility models, control and management," "Social mobile networks and applications" (§0) — only the *third* is even arguably a fit, and only because "social" + "mobile" appear in it; the first two are about controlling/managing mobility in the network, which this paper does not touch. The plan's own §10.2 admits the network angle is "now motivation rather than a measured contribution," which is precisely the definition of out-of-core-scope for a *management* symposium.

3. **The case-for-relevance rests on a single cherry-picked precedent and treats it as license rather than as a bar to clear.** §0 cites "the 2020 Best Paper was a location-based mobility study; a 2021 finalist was a neural mobility-prediction model" and concludes "the paper rests on this mobility-prediction precedent." But (a) the plan gives no evidence those accepted papers were *also* network-contribution-free — a location-prediction paper that ties to handover/paging/load is a very different artifact from one that explicitly disclaims all of it; the precedent may not actually cover this submission's posture. (b) Best-paper outliers are weak evidence of *typical* scope, and the plan itself concedes recent editions "skew to hard networking" (the task brief; echoed by §0 "recent editions skew to hard networking"). (c) Leaning on "MobiWac accepted *a* mobility paper once" is the weakest possible scope argument and signals the authors know the core doesn't fit. A networking reader wants a reason *this* prediction matters *to mobility management*, not a citation that *a* prediction paper once won an award.

**What (if anything) would make it clearly in-scope.** Any *one* of these moves it from "generic ML in a lanyard" toward a defensible MobiWac paper; the first is strongly preferred:

- **Add one real management-side evaluation, even a lightweight simulated one.** Take the model's own outputs and feed them into a concrete management proxy with a *management metric*: e.g., next-region top-k as an anticipatory prefetch/handover-candidate set scored by hit-rate vs. cache/handover-prep cost, or a paging-area / tracking-area cost trade-off (paging signaling vs. miss rate) driven by the predicted region distribution, on the same Gowalla/Istanbul mobility. If the freeze (esp. the pending CA cell, §10.1) won't land in time anyway, this is the lower-risk path and I'd view it more favorably on scope.

# Reviewer 2 — ML methodology & rigor

## Summary

This is a multi-task POI-prediction study (next-category macro-F1 + next-region Acc@10) over five Gowalla states plus Istanbul, with two claims: (1) a check-in-level representation lifts next-category sharply over a place embedding, and (2) one joint model beats a dedicated category model everywhere while staying "non-inferior" on region up to mid-size region counts and trailing 2-3 points at the largest. The plan is unusually disciplined about claim hygiene (the §3 "claim discipline" section and the TOST/Wilcoxon machinery are genuinely above the venue's median), and the leak-control instincts (user-disjoint StratifiedGroupKFold, per-fold train-only transition prior, matched-head ceiling) are the right ones. However, the central Part-2 finding is, by the authors' own admission in §3 and §10, **largely unmeasured under the setup the paper actually adopts**: CA is not run, AL/AZ non-inferiority is carried over from a *different* (non-overlapping) windowing, and the headline numbers re-score at a board that has not frozen. Two of the four advertised baselines that would substantiate the novelty and rebut the prior rejection (CTLE, STAN) are implemented but **not yet scored**, and CTLE's own documented run recipe carried a transductive leak that is only "closed for fold-0." The non-inferiority margin (δ_reg = 2 pp) is internally flagged as a user-confirm parameter and is partly justified by the fact that the claim survives at that margin, which is the exact post-hoc reasoning the pre-registration is supposed to forbid. The science is plausible and the protocol is well-designed on paper, but as currently evidenced the load-bearing claim is a forecast, not a result.

## Sharpest methodological weaknesses

**1. The headline Part-2 claim is asserted across a regime where it has not actually been measured, and the plan knows it (§3 PROVISIONAL block; §10.1).** The abstract, §1 ¶5 contribution 3, §2 Part 2, and §6.2 all state the shape "category +3-4 everywhere; region non-inferior at smaller counts; cost grows with region count, ~2.4-3 at the largest."
