Thesis

Substrate carries, architecture pays. Check-in-level Check2HGI provides a task-asymmetric substrate for joint POI prediction: per-visit context lifts single-task next-category macro-F1 by +14 to +29 pp at every U.S.-state Gowalla split. With Check2HGI fixed, joint MTL over a cross-attention backbone adds a small cat lift at four of five states (and small-significantly negative at AL) while paying a sign-consistent 7 to 17 pp cost on next-region Acc@10 — the textbook MTL tradeoff.

Working title (locked v3, 2026-05-01): Substrate Carries, Architecture Pays: Check-In-Level Embeddings for Multi-Task POI Prediction. See articles/[BRACIS]_*/AGENT.md for the paper-side source-of-truth and AGENT_CONTEXT.md for the long-form briefing.

The textbook MTL tradeoff at every state:

  • Reg: MTL B9 < STL by 7–17 pp at every state, sign-consistent. Significant (p ≤ 1.9e-06) at all five.
  • Cat: MTL B9 > STL at four of five states (AZ/FL/CA/TX, p ≤ 2e-06). AL is small-significantly negative (Δ = −0.78 pp, p = 0.036; magnitude ~1.9 % relative on a 41 % F1 scale).
  • The cross-attention architecture's expressiveness gets spent on cat helps cat (joint training transfers signal to the easier 7-class task) at the cost of the harder ~1k–9k-class region task that already has α·log_T graph prior to learn from.

§0.3 — Joint Δm scoreboard (FL is the lone Pareto-positive cell)

FL Δm-MRR multi-seed (n=25): +2.33% at p ≈ 3×10⁻⁸ (25/25 fold-pairs positive). FL Δm-Acc@10: −1.12% at p ≈ 3×10⁻⁵. The MRR-vs-Acc@10 split at FL is paper-grade in both directions — MTL produces better-ranked region predictions than STL even where raw top-10 is worse. Other states (AL/AZ/CA/TX): single-seed n=5 ceiling, Δm Pareto-negative.

See findings/F50_DELTA_M_FINDINGS_LEAKFREE.md · results/paired_tests/.

§0.4 — Recipe selection (scale-conditional B9 vs H3-alt)

B9 is paper-grade better at all large-scale states (FL/CA/TX). H3-alt is paper-grade better on cat at small states (AL/AZ); reg tied. Narrative: scale-conditional optimal recipe. Full table in RESULTS_TABLE.md §0.4.

Mechanism findings (selected)

  • Substrate task-asymmetry — per-visit context drives the cat lift; region pooling erases the per-visit variance. findings/SUBSTRATE_COMPARISON_FINDINGS.md.
  • F49 — λ=0 cross-attn methodology side-finding: loss-side task_weight=0 ablation does NOT isolate the targeted encoder under cross-attention (silenced encoder co-adapts via attention K/V). Encoder-frozen isolation is required. Generalises beyond our study (MulT, InvPT, HMT-GRN). findings/F49_LAMBDA0_DECOMPOSITION_RESULTS.md, F49_LAMBDA0_DECOMPOSITION_GAP.md.
  • FL Markov-1-region floor — at FL Gowalla, Markov-1-region transitions cover ≥ 85% of validation rows (Acc@10 floor ≈ 65%). Our STAN-Flow exceeds it; MTL trails on Acc@10 but exceeds on Acc@5 + MRR. Honest framing in articles/STATISTICAL_AUDIT.md §7.3.
  • HGI POI2Vec ≈ fclass lookup — diagnosed in the closed hgi_category_injection sub-study. studies/archive/hgi_category_injection/STATUS.md.
  • F-trail journey: the supplementary-material narrative is in MTL_ARCHITECTURE_JOURNEY.md (B3 → F45 → F48-H3-alt → F49 → paper closure). Per-experiment findings: findings/ (66 entries, with topic index in findings/README.md).

Architecture & champion config

MTLnet (src/models/mtl/mtlnet/model.py): task-specific encoders + FiLM modulation + shared 4-block residual backbone + task-specific heads. MTLnetCrossAttn variant adds bidirectional cross-attention between cat and reg streams.

Champion recipe (B9):

mtlnet_crossattn + static_weight(cat=0.75) + next_gru (cat) + next_stan_flow (reg) + per-head LR (cat=1e-3 / reg=3e-3 / shared=1e-3) + cosine + alt-SGD + α-no-WD + per-fold log_T

Full spec in NORTH_STAR.md. Small-state alternative: H3-alt (B9 minus alt-SGD/cosine/α-no-WD; replaced with constant scheduler). Recipe is scale-conditional — see RESULTS_TABLE.md §0.4.

Substrate (Check2HGI): check-in-level hierarchical graph embeddings — same POI visited twice produces different vectors based on user/time/spatial context. Engine code: research/embeddings/check2hgi/. Paper-faithful HGI baseline at research/embeddings/hgi/.
