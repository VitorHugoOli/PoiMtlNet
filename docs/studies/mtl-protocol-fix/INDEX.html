Single-sentence goal
Fix the production joint_canonical_b9 selector bug (CONCERNS.md C21); instrument three-frontier MTL evaluation (best joint + best disjoint + STL ceiling); characterise the residual MTL-vs-STL reg gap that survives the protocol fix across all five states.

§1 — Why this study exists

The closed canonical_improvement study (Tier 1-6, 26 mechanism families, ~40 GPU-h) exhausted the substrate axis on next-region without delivering a paper-grade reg lift. Ceiling Δreg ≤ ±0.8 pp across all variants. Tier 6's load-bearing finding was not a substrate result — it was the discovery that the production joint_canonical_b9 selector at src/training/runners/mtl_cv.py:679 throws away ~10.7 pp of reg-top10 capacity from the canonical Check2HGI shipping recipe alone at FL (matched-protocol single-seed=42 n=5):

Cross-check vs published §0.1 (FL n=20): reg top10 = 63.27 ± 0.10 ≡ the matched-protocol joint_canonical_b9 value within single-seed variance. §0.1 reports joint-best, not the substrate's reg-best capacity.

The next-reg gap in the published canon is therefore ~70 %+ protocol-side, not substrate-side. This study moves the next research axis from substrate to protocol.

§2 — Conceptual scope (the four Qs)

See considerations.md for verbatim user framing. The four questions this study explicitly answers:

  1. Q1 — Why does HGI win STL next-reg despite c2hgi being load-bearing? Two substrates encode two inductive biases; cat is c2hgi-load-bearing (per-visit context), reg is HGI-marginally-load-bearing (POI-stable spatial manifold via Delaunay POI-POI). §0.3 STL reg gap is small (1.6-3.1 pp); §0.1 MTL reg gap to matched-head STL is large (7-17 pp). The MTL-vs-STL component is what this study targets.
  2. Q2 — Beyond protocol/selector, where else could the gap live? Coverage matrix in considerations.md §Q2. This study scopes IN protocol/selector; OUT (→ future_works): MTL loss balancing, MTL architecture revisit, head re-design, window/mask audit, batch class-balance.
  3. Q3 — Report MTL in three frontiers (best disjoint + best joint + STL ceiling). Adopted as the primary methodological deliverable.
  4. Q4 — Confirm STL heads report at per-task best. Phase 0 gate.

§3 — Three-frontier MTL evaluation (the methodological deliverable)

For every MTL model, every state, every task, report three numbers:

FrontierFormula / DefinitionInterpretation
MTL @ best joint joint_geom_simple = sqrt(cat_macro_f1 × reg_top10_acc_indist) — or joint_geom_lift already coded at mtl_cv.py:710 Single-checkpoint deployable number. Replaces the broken joint_canonical_b9.
MTL @ best disjoint cat from cat-best epoch; reg from reg-best epoch (independent across heads) Substrate-capacity ceiling. Diagnostic for what the model could deliver if checkpointing were not constrained.

Phase 1 — F1 fix across 5 states

Hypothesis: the selector bug exists at every state but its magnitude scales with region count (4 700 at FL produces the maximal rare-class-noise impact; ~1 100 at AL produces the minimal).

Method: re-evaluate shipping (canonical Check2HGI + v3c AdamW WD=5e-2 + T3.2 ResLN) at AL/AZ/FL/CA/TX single-seed=42 n=5 ep=50 using existing run dirs in results/check2hgi/{state}/mtlnet_*/. Produce the full three-frontier table.

Expected outcomes:

  • FL Δreg under F1 vs production: +10.7 pp (already confirmed).
  • AL/AZ Δreg under F1 vs production: probably small (region count ~1-1.5 k, less rare-class noise).
  • CA/TX Δreg under F1 vs production: probably medium (6-8 k regions).

Falsifiability gate: if AL/AZ/CA/TX show < 2 pp reg lift under F1, the selector bug is FL-specific and the substrate-axis story at small states stands. Re-opens the residual STL substrate-Δ question.

Phase 1 COMPLETE (closed via Phase 2 P2 multi-seed FL on fresh log_T, 2026-05-20 ~17:00 UTC). Verdict at phase1_phase2_verdict_v6_final.md.

Phase 2 P4 (NEW — emerged during execution) — Frozen-cat horizon test (CA)

P4 COMPLETE (2026-05-20 ~16:00 UTC). Hypothesis test: if MTL reg gap is cat-task negative-transfer, training MTL with cat fully frozen + zero cat-weight should let reg learn to STL ceiling.

Result: HYPOTHESIS FALSIFIED. CA frozen-cat MTL reg per-fold best epoch = 2 (identical to regular CA MTL); peaks at ~50% then crashes after ep 11 — same pattern as cat-active MTL. The MTL backbone architecture itself caps reg learning, NOT cat-task interference.

Implication: redirects next-tier study from loss balancing (which can't fix architectural cap) → architecture revisit (give MTL reg head direct region-emb access, bypassing the shared backbone).

Phase 3 — Residual-gap characterisation

Question: after F1 fix, how much of the §0.1 MTL-vs-STL reg gap survives at each state?

Method: compare Phase 1 per-state MTL @ best joint (new) vs STL ceiling (RESULTS_TABLE §0.1 STL columns). Quantify residual.

Expected outcomes (informed by pre-F1 numbers):

  • FL: pre-F1 gap = 7.34 pp; post-F1 expected ~0 pp (per-task disjoint already at 76 pp vs STL 70.62).
  • AL: pre-F1 gap = 11.04 pp; post-F1 expected ~partial (depends on F1 magnitude at small states).
  • AZ: pre-F1 gap = 12.27 pp; post-F1 expected ~partial.
  • CA: pre-F1 gap = 9.50 pp; post-F1 expected ~partial.
  • TX: pre-F1 gap = 16.59 pp; post-F1 expected ~partial.

The residual quantification produces the brief for the next study:

  • Residual ≤ 2 pp at all states → MTL-vs-STL reg gap is essentially closed by F1; the BRACIS paper headline shifts from "MTL trades reg for cat" to "MTL achieves STL-level reg under the right selector."
  • Residual 2-5 pp at some states → MTL loss-balancing (substrate_adaptive_mtl_balancing.md) is the highest-EV next study.
  • Residual > 5 pp at most states → MTL architecture revisit (mtl_architecture_revisit.md) becomes load-bearing.
  • Residual asymmetric (small at FL/CA/TX, large at AL/AZ) → head-axis (head_window_batch_audit.md) becomes a candidate (the "best head differs across scales" hypothesis).

The paper headline holds: "MTL trades reg for cat at every state, but the F1-fix selector recovers most of the substrate capacity that the production b9 selector wastes." §0.1 v11 paper canon is UNCHANGED; the F1 fix adds a new deployable-selector column without contradicting v11.
