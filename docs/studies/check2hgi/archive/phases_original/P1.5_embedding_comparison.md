# Phase P1.5 — Embedding Comparison (Check2HGI vs POI-level alternatives)

**Goal:** justify the choice of Check2HGI (check-in-level contextual embeddings) over POI-level embeddings for the MTL task pair `{next_category, next_region}`. Answers the reviewer question "why this substrate?"

**Duration:** ~2 h (Alabama only; 1 state × 2 embeddings × 5 folds × 50 epochs with `next_tcn_residual` for compute efficiency).

**Gates:** P1 complete (yes — single-task region baselines frozen on AL at 56.94 ± 4.01 Acc@10 with `next_gru`).

---

## Design decision: which POI-level embedding to compare against?

The check2HGI engine produces *per-check-in* contextual vectors (same POI visited twice → two different vectors). Two POI-level alternatives exist in this repo:

| Candidate | Granularity | Dim | Available? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **POI2HGI** | POI-level, same HGI architecture as Check2HGI | 64 | ✅ in `research/embeddings/poi2hgi/`; files at `output/poi2hgi/<state>/` |
| HGI | POI-level, hierarchical (POI/region) graph infomax | 256 | ✅ in `research/embeddings/hgi/`; files at `output/hgi/<state>/` |

**Adopted:** **POI2HGI** is the fair comparator. It is the same model family and the same dim (64) as Check2HGI — the only variable is *granularity* (check-in vs POI). HGI introduces additional confounds (different architecture, different dim). Run POI2HGI as the primary comparison; HGI can be added later as an extra row if time allows and reviewers push.

## Experiments

| # | State | Embedding | Head | Folds | Epochs | Purpose |
|---|-------|-----------|------|-------|--------|---------|
| P1.5.A | AL | Check2HGI | `next_tcn_residual` | 5 | 50 | Reference (matches P1 TCN, reported Acc@10 = 56.11 ± 4.02) |
| P1.5.B | AL | POI2HGI | `next_tcn_residual` | 5 | 50 | Test: does POI-level do better/worse? |

**Why `next_tcn_residual`, not `next_gru`:** compute efficiency (~20× faster) with statistically equivalent Acc@10 on AL (per C06). One arm is a P1 re-use (free).

**Input modality:** region-emb sequence, consistent with P1 winner. Both embeddings have the same 64-dim region-emb setup.

## Analysis

**Headline numbers:**

| Arm | Embedding | Acc@10 | Acc@10 std |
|---|---|---|---|
| P1.5.A | Check2HGI | 56.11 ± 4.02 | (from `E_confirm_tcn_region.json`) |
| P1.5.B | POI2HGI | **TBD** | TBD |

**Decision rule:**

- If Check2HGI wins by ≥ 2 pp Acc@10 (> std): the paper's substrate claim holds. "Check-in-level contextual embeddings improve the region task over POI-level embeddings of the same family."
- If tied within std: the substrate claim is softer — the paper frames Check2HGI as "at least comparable to POI-level; enables future work on context-aware embeddings" rather than "clearly better."
- If POI2HGI wins by ≥ 2 pp: the paper's framing must change. Check2HGI becomes a design choice motivated by future extensions (encoder enrichment, P6), not by the region-task headline number. This is material enough to pause and reconsider the whole framing before P2 launches.
