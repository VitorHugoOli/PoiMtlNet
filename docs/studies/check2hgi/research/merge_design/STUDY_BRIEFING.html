Check2HGI — Full Picture, HGI Contrast & Merge-Design Study

A meticulous briefing on what Check2HGI is, how it differs structurally from HGI, and what the docs/studies/check2hgi/research/merge_design study attacked in pursuit of an embedding that wins on both next-category and next-region. — Synthesised from the merge_design folder (INDEX, STATE, SUMMARY, MERGE_DESIGN_NOTES, AUDIT_HGI_GAP, DESIGN_*, LEVER_*) and the engine source under research/embeddings/{check2hgi,hgi}/.

TL;DR

Check2HGI is a 4‑level hierarchical contrastive embedding for check‑in events (Check-in → POI → Region → City). It dominates next-category by ~15 pp F1 over HGI but trails it by 2–3 pp Acc@10 on next-region, because its POI representation has no spatial/semantic prior — only the trajectory aggregation provides it.

The merge_design study ran 10+ design variants (A, B, D, E, H, I, J, K, M plus levers 4–6) attempting to inject HGI's POI semantics (POI2Vec / Delaunay graph / hierarchical fclass prior) into Check2HGI without breaking its cat F1. The shipping recommendation is a family (B / H / I / J / M) that all inject POI2Vec at the POI‑pool boundary with the cat path detached. All five close ~80–100 % of the AL/AZ reg gap; at FL the gain shrinks to ~+0.7 pp and no merge variant overtakes HGI. The residual ~1 pp gap on AZ/FL was traced to the training recipe (POI↑POI contrastive boundary, hierarchical fclass L2), but Lever 6 (4th contrastive boundary) and K (Delaunay POI‑POI GCN) were also falsified.

1. What Check2HGI is

Check2HGI = "Check-in to Hierarchical Graph Infomax". It extends the Hierarchical Graph Infomax (HGI) framework one level down the spatial pyramid: instead of treating each POI as a node, it treats every individual check-in event as a node and learns a unique embedding per visit. POI / Region / City levels are produced by aggregation.

The model is fully self-supervised: it never sees a downstream label. Supervision comes from contrastive mutual‑information losses between adjacent levels of the hierarchy — each level must be discriminative of its parent, and the parent must be discriminative of its constituents.

Why per-visit?

Two visits to the same Starbucks — one at 8 am on Monday by user A, one at 10 pm on Saturday by user B — encode different behavioural contexts. HGI would assign them identical vectors (same POI → same node). Check2HGI gives them distinct vectors that fold temporal and trajectory context in.

Implication for downstream tasks. Per‑visit embeddings are perfect for next‑category (the immediate behavioural context is the dominant signal). They are weak for next‑region when the regional pattern is dominated by stable POI semantics (what kind of POI is here, what neighbours it has), which HGI captures via its POI2Vec fclass prior + Delaunay spatial graph. This trade‑off is the entire research problem this study attacks.

2. Architecture & data flow

2.1 The 4‑level hierarchy

┌──────────────┐ │ CITY │ area-weighted sigmoid pool └──────┬───────┘ │ Region2City ┌──────┴───────┐ │ REGIONS │ PMA attention + Region GCN └──────┬───────┘ (reused verbatim from HGI) │ POI2Region ┌──────┴───────┐ │ POIs │ multi-head attention pool └──────┬───────┘ over check-ins of that POI │ Checkin2POI ┌──────┴───────┐ │ CHECK-INS │ 2-layer GCN over user-sequence └──────────────┘ edges (temporal-decay weights)

3. Training objective — 3 boundaries of mutual information

Boundary 1 L_c2p check-in vs its POI (positive) check-in vs random POI (negative) Boundary 2 L_p2r POI vs its region (positive) POI vs random wrong region (negative) Boundary 3 L_r2c region vs city summary (positive) corrupted region vs city (negative) Total L = α_c2p · L_c2p + α_p2r · L_p2r + α_r2c · L_r2c defaults: 0.4 / 0.3 / 0.3

All three losses use a bilinear discriminator score = σ(z₁ᵀ W z₂). Negative sampling is random (Check2HGI dropped HGI's 25 % hard‑negative mining for an O(1) vs O(R) speedup — see Check2HGIModule.py:189-202) and corruption is done at embedding level (a single encoder pass + row‑permute) instead of HGI’s two‑pass feature‑level corruption.

4. Check2HGI vs HGI — the structural diff

Check2HGI

  • Node: single check‑in (event)
  • Features: 11‑dim: cat one‑hot + 4 temporal
  • Edges: user trajectory, temporal decay
  • Hierarchy: 4‑level (adds Check‑in → POI)
  • Boundaries: 3 (c2p, p2r, r2c)
  • POI identity: only emergent from attention pool
  • Spatial signal: none direct (only via region GCN)
  • Best at: next-category

HGI

  • Node: single POI (place)
  • Features: 64‑dim POI2Vec (fclass‑level Node2Vec + hierarchical category L2)
  • Edges: Delaunay triangulation over coords, weighted by distance and a cross‑region penalty w_r
  • Hierarchy: 3‑level
  • Boundaries: 2 explicit (p2r, r2c) at HGI‑train time, but POI2Vec pretraining adds a 3rd boundary (skip‑gram POI↑POI on Node2Vec walks) and a 4th objective (hierarchical category L2 on fclass embeddings). AUDIT_HGI_GAP counts HGI as effectively 4‑boundary; the bare contrastive loop is 2.
  • POI identity: a static lookup (POIs with same fclass share embeddings)
  • Spatial signal: first‑class via Delaunay edges
  • Best at: next-region, POI similarity, fclass clustering

4.1 What HGI has that Check2HGI doesn't (read straight from AUDIT_HGI_GAP.md)

  1. POI2Vec prior at the POI level. A pretrained fclass embedding trained with skip‑gram over Node2Vec walks plus a hierarchical L2 that pulls fclasses toward their parent category (Food ⊇ Coffee, Restaurant, Bar). All POIs sharing an fclass share an identical vector. Check2HGI never sees an fclass label.
  2. Delaunay spatial POI‑POI graph. HGI's GCN passes messages along spatial neighbours weighted by log((1+D¹·⁵)/(1+dist¹·⁵)) · w_r. Check2HGI's graph is temporal, not spatial — POI proximity is only encoded via co‑visit edges.
  3. Hard negative mining (25 % from the 0.6–0.8 similarity band) on the p2r boundary. Check2HGI dropped this for speed.
  4. 4th contrastive boundary (implicit) via POI2Vec's skip‑gram — a POI↑POI positive pair is whatever sits within a random walk window. Check2HGI has 3 boundaries; no POI↑POI.

5. The problem this study attacks

Stated in STATE.md:

Merge HGI's POI semantics into Check2HGI without breaking next-category, and overcome HGI on next-region (and eventually next-POI). Produce a single embedding usable across other tasks — not two disjoint engines.

The naive attempt (concatenate POI2Vec into the 11‑dim check‑in input — the "POI2Vec input probe") closes 66–82 % of the reg gap but tanks cat by ~7–9 pp in all 5 folds (Wilcoxon p=0.0312). The two engines have opposing inductive biases; shared‑encoder optimisation collapses to a compromise that hurts both.

The study therefore enumerates merge designs of escalating ambition and tests each on AL, AZ and FL using leak‑free per‑fold transition logs.

The unifying recipe that works

checkin_emb = CheckinEncoder(canonical_11d_features, edge_index) poi_emb_canonical = Checkin2POI(checkin_emb, checkin_to_poi) ← cat path poi_emb_for_reg = poi_emb_canonical.detach() ← stop gradient on cat + γ · Linear_64→64( POI2Vec[poi] ) ← B (frozen) + nn.Embedding(N_poi, 64)[poi] ← H/J (learnable) + U[poi] · V ← I (LoRA r=8) + λ_d · cosine_distill(P(poi), POI2Vec) ← M (loss only) region_emb = POI2Region(poi_emb_for_reg) ← reg path L_c2p uses poi_emb_canonical (cat supervision → encoder) L_p2r uses poi_emb_for_reg (reg supervision → never encoder) L_r2c uses region_emb

The load‑bearing trick across B/H/I/J/M: the cat path is byte‑identical to canonical c2hgi. POI2Vec only enters after the POI pool, never in the check‑in features, and its gradient is gated off the encoder by .detach(). This is why none of these designs regresses cat. Designs A and E failed precisely because they put POI2Vec into the input or let it backprop through the encoder.

What the "merge" actually is, plainly

HGI's POI2Vec is a fclass lookup table — all POIs sharing a functional class share an identical 64‑dim vector (Phase 3d of HGI's pipeline is literally poi_emb[i] = fclass_embeddings[poi.fclass[i]]). B/H/I/J/M all import that table at the POI‑pool boundary, behind a stop‑gradient on the cat path. That is why fclass linear probe jumps from 4 % to 98 % almost mechanically — it is substitution, not learning. By the same token, the merge inherits HGI's linear separability for fclass but not the POI‑geometry that Node2Vec walks on the Delaunay graph induced — which is exactly what the kNN‑Jaccard@10 = 0.07–0.12 vs HGI is telling us. The merge family is closer to "c2hgi + HGI's fclass dictionary" than to "a true co‑training of both engines".

7. Empirical findings (the mechanism analysis)

  1. The reg gap is fclass‑cluster signal at the POI level, not per-visit noise. The postpool diagnostic (POI-mean → region‑mean post-hoc pool) gave zero lift, falsifying the "check-ins are too noisy" hypothesis. The POI2Vec input probe gave reg lift but tanked cat, confirming the signal source.
  2. The cat‑vs‑reg trade‑off is input‑side, not gradient‑side. Design E proved that stop‑gradient projector heads do not recover cat once POI2Vec is in the input — the GCN propagates POI features regardless of which losses backprop. This rules out a whole family of optimisation patches (PCGrad, GradNorm, adapters) applied to POI2Vec‑augmented input.
  3. Cat path must be byte‑identical to canonical to avoid regression. B and H succeed only because canonical 11‑dim input is preserved end‑to‑end on the cat path.
  4. fclass probe is a necessary generality gate. Canonical c2hgi: cat F1 40.76 BUT fclass probe 4 %. The merge family recovers HGI‑grade fclass (98 %) at no cat cost. This is a generality benefit (transferable POI semantics), not necessarily a next‑task benefit.
  5. kNN‑Jaccard@10 vs HGI is the harder generality test. B/H/I/J/M score 0.07–0.12 — they recover linear separability but not POI geometry. If a downstream task needs HGI‑style POI similarity retrieval, the merged engine is not interchangeable.
  6. Heterograph leaks under unsupervised pretraining. Adding POI nodes carrying POI2Vec + reverse visits edges creates a 2-hop path from target POI back to the last check-in. D's +32 pp cat lift is mostly leak amplification, not generalisation.
  7. Learnable POI table > frozen residual is at best 0.5 pp. H beats B by +0.86 pp on AL reg and +0.05 on AZ reg — both within 1σ. The dominant factor is the POI2Vec init, not the learnable update.
  8. FL doesn't scale the win. All 6 merge variants converge within ±0.25 pp on FL reg (82.2–82.45 against the original stale baseline; 0.69–0.70 leak‑free). With more data, canonical's POI representations are sufficient for reg; POI2Vec adds nothing measurable. Only H and J cross strict‑Wilcoxon over canonical at FL; none beats HGI.

Phase 11 substrate audit (2026-05-07)

A final pass questioned methodology — the c2hgi training recipe itself. All four candidate fixes (S1: c2p hard‑negatives, S4: DGI corrupted-feature c2p negatives, S3-a: auxiliary Checkin2Region 4th boundary, S3-b: Checkin2Region as primary region pathway including a V2-c variant with per‑check‑in POI2Vec anchor) FALSIFIED. Headlines:

  • S1 and S4 regress.
  • S3‑a v1+v2 collapse to uniform attention (supervision redundant with the 3‑boundary path).
  • S3‑b V1 lands at canonical levels — the pooler architecture buys ~0.1 pp; the J→canonical gap is POI2Vec, not pooling.
  • S3‑b V2‑c: catastrophic on AL (Δ=−9.95 pp), mild on AZ. State‑asymmetric: anchor collapses small per-region pools.
  • PMA is structurally a learnable mean-pool (PR‑norm=1.0 throughout) — the regional aggregator has less expressive freedom than its attention machinery suggests.

9. Verdict & open questions

What the study delivers (the realised contribution)

  • A Pareto improvement over canonical c2hgi on cat + reg + POI semantic generality, via a single‑engine merge. B/H/I/J/M are statistically indistinguishable under the user's strict Wilcoxon dual-axis gate at n=5; pick by axis priority:
    PriorityShipWhy
    Cat F1MOnly design Wilcoxon-strict on cat at both AL and AZ.
    AL reg vs HGIJStrict reg AL win, +0.10 pp vs HGI nominal. Does not generalise (AZ −1.22).
    Param cost / scale to FL/CA/TXILoRA r=8 — ~8× fewer extra params than H. Functionally B with capacity.
    Simplicity / productionBCleanest mechanism, smallest footprint, no hybrid outperforms it at the strict gate.
    The headline paper claim should be the recipe (POI2Vec at POI‑pool, cat detached) rather than any one variant.
  • POI2Vec at the POI‑pool boundary, cat‑path detached is the load‑bearing pattern. All five (B/H/I/J/M) pass dominance at AL/AZ; at FL only H and J cross strict Wilcoxon over canonical.
  • fclass linear probe goes 4 % → 98 %: HGI‑grade POI semantic recovery at no cat cost.
  • Pre‑registered generality probes (fclass linear, kNN‑Jaccard, region category‑mix regression) are now part of the eval contract — cat F1 alone over‑fits.

What the study does not deliver

  • No design overcomes HGI on next‑region at AZ or FL. AL crosses nominally with J (+0.10 pp) but the win does not generalise.
  • Next‑POI (the user's ultimate goal): J closes 77 % of the gap on AL/AZ but does not overcome HGI; FL not measured. The "overcome HGI on next‑POI" thesis claim is unsupported.
  • The residual ~1 pp reg gap at AZ/FL has no structural explanation left. Features (POI2Vec) are injected, structure (Delaunay) is added (K), a 4th boundary is added (L6), the consumer (POI2Region) is at/past optimal, the methodology (Phase 11) was audited. All falsified. "This gap is below the study's architectural resolution at n=5 folds."

    The stronger implication. If K=J, L6=J, and the 5-step Phase 11 substrate audit all returned null, then HGI's structural edge is not its Delaunay edges, its 4th boundary, or its set transformer — it is encoded inside the frozen POI2Vec output itself, and importing that output (which is exactly what B/H/I/J/M do) already extracts ≥99 % of what c2hgi's downstream loss can absorb. The residual ~1 pp on AZ/FL is then most plausibly HGI's reg head receiving a stronger gradient pathway on the same POI2Vec (HGI trains POI→Region→City directly, without c2hgi's 3-boundary distraction including the per-visit L_c2p), or alternatively that the POI2Vec geometry itself would shift if it were jointly retrained with c2hgi's losses (see §10 push‑next #4 — this is the one merge mechanism the study never tested).

10. Critical assessment

Strengths

  1. Mechanism-first design ladder. A → E → B → H/I/J/M follows ablation logic, not "throw architectures at the wall". Each design either confirms the previous lesson or escalates one specific axis.
  2. Strong falsification discipline. When K, L6, T1, T2, T2½, T3, S1, S4, S3‑a, S3‑b were null, they were written up as falsified hypotheses, not glossed over. The advisor‑reframing pivot (2026‑05‑06) is a healthy reaction to convergence‑saturation.
  3. Leak hygiene. The April leaky region_transition_log.pt was caught and the entire FL design table re-run on per-fold leak-free logs — this kind of post-hoc rebuild is rare and saved a misleading +13 pp headline from reaching the paper.
  4. Two generality probes (fclass linear, kNN-Jaccard) added before the comparisons. Without them, canonical c2hgi's 4 % fclass-probe disaster would have stayed invisible.

Weaknesses & risks

  1. n=5 folds is power‑limited. The Wilcoxon strict floor at p=1/32 = 0.0312 means designs that are actually different by 0.1–0.3 pp can be indistinguishable. The user's preferred dual‑axis gate fails on every design — an n=10 or multi‑seed protocol is needed to settle J vs B vs M.
  2. FL is a different regime, not a scaled-up AL. Reg gain shrinks from +2.3 pp → +0.7 pp; cat F1 at FL was never re-measured under the leak-free protocol; the "dominance prediction at FL is non-inferiority pending verification" is not closed.
  3. The kNN‑Jaccard 0.07–0.12 vs HGI means the merged POI vectors are not geometrically interchangeable with HGI's — downstream tasks that depend on POI‑similarity retrieval would not be unaffected. M's cosine distillation was the obvious lever and it didn't move kNN‑Jaccard (0.110/0.072 ≈ B). M's win is not explained by its stated mechanism.
  4. POI‑level metrics are largely absent. Per AUDIT_HGI_GAP.md §3, the entire merge family is evaluated on next-region and next-category; next-POI — the user's actual research goal — has no head wired into the pipeline. T1 ran a probe but the production pipeline still has no next-POI evaluation path.
  5. Phase 11 finding that PMA collapses to mean‑pool (PR‑norm=1.0) deserves more weight in the writeup — if the "set transformer" isn't doing set-transformer work, the residual gap may be in choices upstream of POI2Region rather than inside it.
  6. Hard‑negatives were dropped wholesale in Check2HGI for speed. Phase 11 S1 tried adding them back at the c2p boundary and saw regression, but a more measured re-introduction at p2r (matching HGI's 25 % hard‑neg rule on the actual region boundary) was not isolated.
  7. The "ship B" recommendation is correct but understated. If B is statistically indistinguishable from H/I/J/M at the user's gate, the paper claim should be the recipe (POI2Vec at POI-pool, cat detached) rather than any specific variant. Otherwise the paper risks litigating between equivalent designs.

Where to push next

  1. Increase n. A 10‑seed protocol on B/H/I/J/M at AL+AZ would resolve the dominance gate at p<0.01 instead of p=0.0312.
  2. Build a next‑POI head into the merge pipeline. Until that runs, every claim about "overcoming HGI on next-POI" is unverified.
  3. Re-introduce hard negatives at p2r only (not c2p where S1 failed) — this matches HGI's reg-side mechanism exactly without touching the cat path.
  4. Treat the residual 1 pp gap as evidence that HGI's POI2Vec already encodes everything c2hgi's reg path can reach. The remaining structural difference is HGI's training distribution (Node2Vec walks on Delaunay), not its inference graph. Re-training POI2Vec on a c2hgi-aware objective (rather than freezing HGI's pretrained output) is the one merge mechanism the study never tested.
