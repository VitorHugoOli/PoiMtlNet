MTL Exploration — State of the Current MTL Model

A consolidated, source-traced report on every backbone, loss, optimizer, head, schedule, and ablation we have run on the Check2HGI MTL pair (next_category + next_region), and why the current config settled where it did. As of 2026-05-14 paper-canonical numbers = RESULTS_TABLE.md §0 v11 (2026-05-02)

0. TL;DR + scope

Numbers discipline. Three eras coexist across the F-trail; only one is paper-canonical.
  • superseded Pre-leak-fix era (≤ 2026-04-29). F48-H3-alt “AL +6.25 pp MTL > STL on reg”, F49 “+6.48 pp architecture-dominant”, the “MTL exceeds STL” champion framing — all leak artefacts of the full-data region_transition_log.pt (~13–27 pp inflation, substrate-asymmetric).
  • historical Leak-free single-seed (2026-04-30, F51). B9 emerges; numbers single-seed only.
  • paper-canonical v11 (2026-05-02). docs/results/RESULTS_TABLE.md §0 — multi-seed (n = 20 across all 5 states on §0.1), leak-free, per-fold seed-tagged log_T.
Every “current state” number below traces to v11. Leak-era numbers appear only inside the journey narrative and are flagged.

The honest thesis (post leak-free)

The paper does not claim that joint MTL lifts both heads above their STL ceilings. The pre-leak-fix “bidirectional” thesis was abandoned in the 2026-05-01 closure. What survives is:

  1. C1 — substrate task-asymmetry. Check-in-level Check2HGI lifts next_category macro-F1 by +14.5 to +29 pp over POI-stable HGI at every state (paired Wilcoxon p = 0.0312 each). Per-visit context = ~72–90% of that gap (CH19).
  2. C2 — classic MTL tradeoff. With substrate fixed, MTL nudges cat above the matched-head STL baseline at 4 of 5 states (Δ ∈ [+1.20, +1.89] pp, p ≤ 2e-06) and pays a sign-consistent reg cost at every state (Δ ∈ [−7.34, −16.59] pp, p ≤ 1.9e-06).
  3. C3 — methodological note (F49 Layer 2). Under cross-attention, loss-side task_weight=0 is unsound because the silenced encoder co-adapts via K/V. Encoder-frozen isolation is the only clean architectural ablation. Applies beyond this study (MulT, InvPT, HMT-GRN).

2.3 Key data-flow facts

  • Two encoders, no parameter sharing across tasks. Each task has its own MLP encoder (2 layers, 256→256), each cross-attn block has its own FFN per task. Information is shared only via the cross-attention K/V exchange.
  • Re-zero padding before the heads. After encoder + cross-attn + LN, originally-zero pad steps become non-zero (bias additions). Sequential heads (GRU, LSTM, TCN, Transformer) detect padding via x.abs().sum(-1)==0. We re-mask with the original pad indicator (MTLnetCrossAttn.forward lines 268–272).
  • Aux side-channel for last_region_idx. src/data/aux_side_channel.py publishes per-batch tensors that the reg head reads inside forward without changing the trainer signature. Gated by membership set _HEADS_REQUIRING_AUX_MTL = {"next_getnext_hard", "next_getnext_hard_hsm"} in src/data/folds.py (a hardcoded equality check here was the F50-era silent bug that broke HSM — add new heads here when applicable).
  • Mixed-batch iteration. The two tasks have different dataset sizes (cat = POI-level, region = sequence-level). The trainer cycles the shorter loader to match the longer; under B9 alt-SGD each task gets its own optimizer step on its own batch.
  • 5-fold StratifiedGroupKFold, user-disjoint. groups = userid prevents user-level leakage; seed = 42 default, multi-seed pool over {0,1,7,100} for n=20.

6. LR schedule / optimizer recipes (the actual lever)

8.1 The α scalar — smallest parameter with biggest effect

final_logits = stan_logits + α · log_T[last_region_idx]
                              ↑
α: nn.Parameter(0.1) at init, grows to ~1.77–1.81 at convergence (18× amplification).

α is the single learnable scalar that fuses the STAN sequence model with the (legacy POI-trajectory) graph prior. Its growth requires sustained LR ≥ 2e-3 — the entire F44–F48 disjoint-LR story is α-driven. In B9 we additionally peel α out of AdamW's weight_decay group (α-no-WD) because L2 shrinkage was clipping its growth.

9. Head × backbone co-adaptation

The standalone-vs-MTL inversion is itself a load-bearing study finding:

  • Standalone winners do not survive MTL. category_dcn (F1=0.728 standalone) is unused in the current MTL; next_tcn_residual (F1=0.244 standalone, best on the legacy 7-class next pair) is not used as the reg head.
  • Backbone shapes the head's job. Under cross-attn the cat head receives a cross-task-modulated sequence representation, not a static POI embedding. The optimal head class shifts.
  • F49 silent gradient finding. Even when category_weight=0 in the loss, the cat encoder still receives gradient through cross_ba's K/V projections. The cat encoder is implicitly trained as a reg-helper. This means “disable cat to ablate cat’s effect” is unsound under cross-attn — you must encoder-freeze. paper-grade methodological contribution

10. Decisive ablations that locked the recipe

Three orthogonal negative controls bracket H3-alt as the unique design in its lever space (F48-H3-alt era):

Additional ablations at the post-F50 level:

  • F50 P1 — disable cross-attn entirely. Pure parallel encoders + final LN + heads. Tests whether cross-attn is contributing or hurting at FL. (Plumbed via disable_cross_attn flag.)
  • F52 P5 — identity cross-attn. Cross-attention output forced to zero; per-task FFNs + LNs still run. Decomposes “mixing” vs “structure” contributions of the cross-attn block. Result: mixing is depth-conditional at FL — alive at num_crossattn_blocks=3 (Pareto-trade), breaks cat at depth 4.
  • F50 P2 — detach cross-attn K/V. .detach() on K/V to block silent gradient (the F49 mechanism). Direct test of the cross-attn methodology contribution.
  • F50 D1 — freeze α. freeze_alpha=True, alpha_init=0.0 disables the graph prior. Tests how much of STL's 82.44 (FL) ceiling comes from the prior vs the STAN encoder. The α component dominates.
  • F51 Tier 2 capacity sweep (21 smokes across 7 dims). B9 is locally optimal in 5/7 capacity dimensions. Cat width-stability cliff at shared_layer_size 384/512 — wider shared backbone breaks cat without affecting reg.
  • F50 T1.2 HSM (hierarchical softmax). Empirically falsified head-capacity-mismatch as the FL flaw — the structural cost lives in the shared layer, not the head's output cardinality.
  • F50 T0 Δm decomposition. Maninis CVPR 2019 / Vandenhende TPAMI 2021 joint score; refuted “cat-supervision transfer” story at all 5 states.

12. The transition-prior leak — reframe history

This is the single biggest finding in the study. The α · log_T transition-prior in next_stan_flow was originally built from full-data region_transition_log.pt. Direct measurement on FL fold 1 showed:
  • Prior-only val top10 with full log_T = 90.23% vs per-fold log_T = 80.04% — 10.19 pp gap from log_T alone.
  • α grows from 0.1 (init) to ~1.77–1.81 (converged) — 18× amplification.
  • Trained-model gap at convergence ≈ ~13–17 pp inflation on reg numbers.
The audit's original estimate of 0.5–2 pp was off by ~10× because it assumed α stays near its init. Trainable val-aware priors are worse than static leaks because the model learns to amplify them.

Two paper-reshaping reframes followed:

  1. F49 “AL +6.48 pp architecture-dominant” was a leak artefact. Under symmetric leak-free measurement AL's reg pattern matches every other state — sign-consistent negative.
  2. CH18-reg “MTL is substrate-specific” was a leak artefact. Substrate-asymmetric leakage (worse for C2HGI than HGI) inverted the substrate ordering on reg. Under leak-free measurement, HGI is at parity (CA/TX) or marginally ahead (AL/AZ/FL).

Additional leak hardening: per-fold log_T files are now region_transition_log_seed{S}_fold{N}.pt with seed in the filename; trainer hard-fails on missing or legacy unseeded files. Without this hardening the F51 multi-seed sweep would have leaked ~9 pp on every seed ≠ 42.

13. Open work + gaps

13.1 Active follow-up studies (already chartered)

  • docs/studies/canonical_improvement/ — 18-experiment slate across 5 tiers; targets canonical Check2HGI improvement. T1.1 leak audit → T1.3 α sweep → T1.5 optimizer hygiene → ... → T5 stretch goals. Stop-gate after Tier 1 if α sweep + optimizer hygiene closes the AZ reg gap to within 1 pp of HGI.
  • docs/studies/merge_design/ — Designs A-M / Levers 1-6 / Phase 11. Targets merge-family substrate (concatenations of C2HGI with POI2Vec / HGI). Several formulations falsified (S1 hard negs, S3-a 4th boundary, S3-b V2-c per-check-in POI2Vec anchor, S4 corrupted negs).
  • docs/studies/hgi_category_injection/ — CLOSED, falsified 2026-05-04 on AZ. Held pending FL/CA/TX revisit. Read STATUS.md.

13.2 Identified flaws not yet chartered as a study

  • Architectural cost monotone-ish in region cardinality. Δ_reg = −11 pp (AL) → −12 pp (AZ) → −7 pp (FL) → −10 pp (CA) → −17 pp (TX). The strongest cost is at TX. F51 Tier 2 confirmed capacity scaling cannot rescue it. The cross-attention shared layer pays a price that grows with reg head's output cardinality — no current variant closes it.
  • No MTL > STL on reg, anywhere, under leak-free. The classic tradeoff stands. There is no “dual win” recipe in the searched space (~50 ablations across heads, losses, schedules, capacity).
  • Cat width-stability cliff. shared_layer_size ∈ {384, 512} with num_crossattn_blocks=4, crossattn_ffn_dim=1024 breaks cat without affecting reg. Wider shared backbone is forbidden under current head choice.
  • FAMO and Aligned-MTL untested leak-free. Launchers exist (F50 T1.3/T1.4); never run under per-fold seed-tagged log_T. Expected value modest (no MTL balancer beats equal_weight on this problem) but the closure would be honest.
  • Crossstitch backbone never head-to-head. src/models/mtl/mtlnet_crossstitch/ is scaffolded; no leak-free 5-fold run vs mtlnet_crossattn.
  • Cheapest experiment in this doc — flat single-check-in cat ablation. Run STL next_gru on flat [B, 1, 64] Check2HGI input (no sequence) at the same fold protocol. Would isolate whether the +10 to +23 pp lift over the linear probe is sequence-driven or capacity-driven. Detailed motivation in §14.2.

13.3 Methodological loose ends (paper-side)

  • F49 Layer 2 (cross-attn task_weight=0 unsoundness) applies to MulT, InvPT, HMT-GRN and any cross-task interaction MTL with task-weight ablations. Worth a follow-up methods note.
  • Per-fold seed-tagged log_T is now mandatory; the gate in src/data/folds.py::_HEADS_REQUIRING_AUX_MTL needs every new α·log_T-style head added explicitly. Easy to miss; recommend converting to a duck-typed check (e.g. hasattr(head, 'log_T')).
  • The standalone-vs-MTL inversion on heads is not yet a single consolidated paper claim. The ordering reversal (best standalone heads ≠ best MTL heads) is a publishable observation in its own right.

13.4 Documentation gaps observed during this audit

  • docs/context/MTL_ARCHITECTURES.md still quotes fusion-study legacy CGC/MMoE/DSelectK joint numbers (10ep / 1-fold on the legacy 7+7 pair). These can mislead a fresh reader into thinking those backbones are still competitive on the current check2hgi pair (they are not, but the comparison was never re-run on the current task). Add a leading caveat or move to an archive section.
  • docs/context/TASKS.md describes the legacy task pair (category + next-category, both 7 classes), not the current check2hgi pair (next_category + next_region). The check2hgi task pair is described inside AGENT_CONTEXT.md §Task pair but not in the canonical context/ folder. Worth a refresh.
  • docs/COLAB_GUIDE.md and experiments/check2hgi_up/run_mtl_b3.py still bake in the predecessor B3 recipe (--max-lr 0.003, no per-head LR). Tracked as camera-ready follow-up but easy to forget.

14. Is the input data the right fit for the model?

Short answer: mostly yes, with one open question.

14.1 Where the input fits the model well

  • Reg task (next_region). Sequence input with a 9-step window over region embeddings + last_region_idx aux channel is exactly the input shape next_stan_flow needs: STAN expects a temporal sequence; the α·log_T prior only fires given the last observed region. The window=9 stride=9 non-overlap design is conservative but defensible — it avoids overlap-driven leakage. The per-fold seed-tagged log_T closes the val-train transition leak.
  • Cross-attn match. Two streams with the same sequence shape [B, 9, 64] but different modalities (per-visit check-in vs per-region) is the natural input for bidirectional cross-attention. Per-task encoders + content-based mixing is exactly the inductive bias the architecture provides.
  • Substrate carries cat. CH19 shows ~72–90% of the cat lift over HGI is per-visit context, which is what Check2HGI explicitly encodes. The cat task is being fed the signal it actually needs.

14.2 Where the input may be over-engineered for the cat task

The under-explored question. The cat task (next_category, 7-class macro-F1) is fed a [B, 9, 64] sequence and processed through a 2-layer GRU. But CH19 (RESULTS_TABLE.md §0.7) shows the per-visit Check2HGI substrate alone carries 64–90% of the cat lift. The substrate linear-probe numbers from RESULTS_TABLE.md §0.6 hint at what a static cat predictor can already do:

The +10 to +23 pp gap from linear probe to sequence head is real and growing with state size — the sequence is contributing something at scale. But this comparison conflates (a) sequence information and (b) end-to-end fine-tuning of a richer head. A cleaner ablation would feed the cat head a flat single check-in from the same Check2HGI substrate at a comparably-tuned non-linear (MLP) head — that would isolate whether the 9-step sequence carries the lift or whether non-linearity alone is the lever. That experiment has not been run.

14.3 Where the input is a known mismatch for the reg task

  • Region embedding input. The reg head consumes a 9-step sequence of region embeddings (mean-aggregated over POIs in each region), not the underlying POI/check-in embeddings. This is the configuration committed in B9 (--task-b-input-type region) but the underlying check-in substrate carries more discriminative per-visit context (CH19). Whether this aggregation is the right tradeoff for the reg head is not separately ablated.
  • Per-fold transition matrix granularity. log_T is built per fold from training rows only. At small states (AL, ~1k regions) some target regions have ≤10 transitions in training — the prior is noisy. Per-fold log_T smoothing has not been tested.

14.4 Bottom-line recommendation

  1. The current input pipeline matches the current model: the reg head needs the sequence + last_region channel; the cat head needs the per-visit substrate. Cross-attn is the right architecture for two same-shape different-modality inputs.
  2. Worth a cheap ablation: run STL next_gru on flat single-check-in [B, 1, 64] Check2HGI input (no sequence) at the same fold protocol. Compares against the existing 9-step STL next_gru. If the lift over linear probe is sequence-driven, the gap should shrink dramatically. If it's mostly capacity-driven, it should hold.
  3. Region-input vs check-in-input for the reg head was the P4 winner, but tested under leaky log_T. A leak-free re-comparison would close the loop.
